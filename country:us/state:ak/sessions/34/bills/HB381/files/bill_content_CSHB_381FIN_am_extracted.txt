The abatement period 16 begins on the effective date of this section and ends on the earlier of 17 (1) the day after the natural gas project achieves a throughput of 18 500,000,000 cubic feet of natural gas a day, calculated as a rolling average over a 19 consecutive 30-day period; or 20 (2) five years after the date of commencement of commercial 21 operations of the natural gas project. 22 (b) A natural gas project is eligible for the tax abatement under this section 23 only if the department determines that the project is eligible under AS 43.59.025. 24 Sec. 43.59.020. Imposition of alternative volumetric tax. (a) The owner of 25 property subject to tax under this section shall pay an alternative volumetric tax on the 26 throughput of the property. The alternative volumetric tax applies beginning on the 27 day after the expiration of the abatement period under AS 43.59.010. 28 (b) The volumetric tax is the sum of the amounts calculated under this 29 subsection for each component of a natural gas project. The tax for a component is 30 calculated by multiplying the tax rate for the component, set out under (c) of this 31 section, by the component weight, calculated under (d) of this section, by the total 01 units of component throughput, defined under (e) of this section, for the tax period. 02 (c) The tax rate is 03 (1) $0.06 for a gas pipeline component; 04 (2) $0.13 for a gas treatment plant and carbon capture facility 05 component; 06 (3) $0.13 for a liquefied natural gas plant component. 07 (d) The component weight is the capital expenditures for a completed 08 component divided by the total capital expenditures for all completed components. 09 The department shall calculate the component weights upon commencement of 10 commercial operations of each major phase of the project. The department shall 11 calculate the final and fixed component weight upon commencement of commercial 12 operations of the project. Each time a component weight is calculated under this 13 subsection, the department shall provide the revised component weight to each 14 taxpayer under this section and to a municipality collecting the tax due under this 15 section. 16 (e) A unit of component throughput is 1,000 cubic feet of natural gas. 17 (f) Beginning after the first year the tax applies to throughput of a natural gas 18 project, the tax rate for throughput under (c) of this section shall be adjusted on 19 January 1 of each year for inflation, using 100 percent of the average of the annual 20 change over the preceding five calendar years in the Consumer Price Index for all 21 urban consumers for urban Alaska, as determined by the United States Department of 22 Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, the annual adjustment under this 23 subsection must increase the rates by at least one percent and not more than two 24 percent. 25 (g) A natural gas project is subject to the alternative volumetric tax levied 26 under this section only if the department determines that the project is eligible under 27 AS 43.59.025. 28 (h) The tax levied under this section is in place of 29 (1) all state taxes levied on taxable property, including property used 30 or committed by contract or other agreement for use in the natural gas project; 31 (2) taxes levied under AS 43.56.010; and 01 (3) taxes levied under AS 29.45.080. 02 (i) An owner of property subject to tax under this section shall, on or before 03 the last day of each month, file a return with the department and with each 04 municipality collecting tax under this section. The return must state the throughput, in 05 cubic feet of natural gas for each day, for each component of property subject to tax 06 for the month preceding the month in which the return is due and include an 07 installment payment for the month of the return. An installment payment is considered 08 delinquent if the payment is not received by the department on or before the last day 09 of each month. 10 (j) The tax levied under this section is due annually, on the calendar year. The 11 owner of the property shall, on or before April 30 each year, pay any remaining tax 12 due under this section for tax accruing from throughput in the previous calendar year. 13 A tax payment under this subsection is considered delinquent if the payment is not 14 received by the department on or before April 30 each year. 15 (k) Notwithstanding AS 43.05.220, if a tax payment or installment payment 16 required under this section is delinquent, the department or a municipality shall assess 17 a penalty of 15 percent of the amount of delinquent taxes and interest on the 18 delinquent taxes, exclusive of penalty, at the rate specified in AS 43.05.225. 19 Sec. 43.59.025. Eligibility. (a) A natural gas project is eligible for the tax 20 abatement under AS 43.59.010 and the alternative volumetric tax under AS 43.59.020 21 only if the department determines that the plans for the project meet the requirements 22 of this section. 23 (b) To be eligible under this section, plans for the project must include a spur 24 line meeting the requirements of this subsection that serves the City of Fairbanks and 25 the Fairbanks North Star Borough. The spur line must 26 (1) have sufficient capacity to serve reasonably projected residential, 27 commercial, and industrial demand in the Interior area of the state; 28 (2) be scheduled to begin operations within two years after the 29 commencement of commercial operations of a major component of the natural gas 30 project; 31 (3) be designed to connect with local distribution infrastructure 01 capable of delivering natural gas to the City of Fairbanks and the surrounding urban 02 area; 03 (4) be designed and operated to deliver gas at the lowest reasonable 04 cost consistent with safe and reliable service; and 05 (5) allocate costs, including capital, financing, and construction costs, 06 (A) across all consumers systemwide, including export 07 consumers and consumers in the area from the North Slope to the Southcentral 08 regions of the state; costs related to financing or construction of the spur line 09 may not be allocated solely to the Interior area of the state; and 10 (B) justly, reasonably, and not unduly discriminatorily. 11 (c) If the department determines that the requirements of (b) of this section 12 have been met, the department shall issue a written determination that the natural gas 13 project is eligible for the tax abatement under AS 43.59.010 and the alternative 14 volumetric tax under AS 43.59.020. 15 Sec. 43.59.030. Collection and allocation of alternative tax. (a) The 16 department shall levy and collect the alternative volumetric tax imposed by this 17 chapter that is allocated to the state. Unless otherwise elected by the municipality 18 under (d) of this section, a municipality that is not in the unorganized borough may 19 levy and collect the portion of the alternative volumetric tax imposed by this chapter 20 that is allocated to the municipality. The amount of tax allocated to each municipality 21 and to the unorganized borough is determined under (b) of this section. 22 (b) Each month, the department shall determine the amount of tax allocated to 23 each municipality and to the unorganized borough, as follows: 24 (1) for the portion of the tax levied on the gas pipeline component 25 under AS 43.59.020(c)(1), adjusted by its component weight under AS 43.59.020(d), 26 (A) 50 percent of the tax is allocated to the areas of the state 27 through which a gas pipeline runs; the portion allocated to the state is equal to 28 the proportion of the gas pipeline in the unorganized borough, and the portion 29 allocated to each municipality that is not in the unorganized borough is equal 30 to the proportion of the gas pipeline in the municipality; to determine the 31 proportional distribution under this subparagraph, the length of pipeline in the 01 unorganized borough or a municipality that is not in the unorganized borough 02 is divided by the total length of the pipeline; and 03 (B) 50 percent of the tax is allocated to the state; each year, the 04 legislature may appropriate the amount allocated to the state under this 05 subparagraph to the municipalities, communities, and reserves in the 06 unorganized borough, distributed on a per capita basis under AS 29.60.860; 07 (2) for the portion of the tax levied on the gas treatment plant and 08 carbon capture facility component under AS 43.59.020(c)(2), adjusted by its 09 component weight under AS 43.59.020(d), 10 (A) 93 percent of the tax is allocated to a municipality that is 11 not in the unorganized borough where the gas treatment plant and carbon 12 capture facility is located; 13 (B) 7 percent of the tax is allocated to the state; 14 (3) for the portion of the tax levied on the liquefied natural gas plant 15 component under AS 43.59.020(c)(3), adjusted by its component weight under 16 AS 43.59.020(d), 17 (A) 93 percent of the tax is allocated to a municipality that is 18 not in the unorganized borough where the liquefied natural gas plant is located; 19 (B) 7 percent of the tax is allocated to the state. 20 (c) Each month, the department shall report to a municipality collecting tax 21 under this section the amount of tax allocated to the municipality for the preceding 22 month. 23 (d) A municipality may, by ordinance, elect for the department to collect, on 24 behalf of the municipality, the portion of the tax levied under this chapter that is 25 allocated to the municipality. 26 Sec. 43.59.040. Administrative appeals; distraint of property. (a) A 27 decision by the department regarding the imposition or calculation of the tax levied 28 under AS 43.59.020 may be appealed to the department for an informal conference 29 under AS 43.05.240, and a final decision may be appealed to the office of 30 administrative hearings under AS 43.05.405. 31 (b) The remedy of distraint of property set out in AS 43.20.270 applies to the 01 tax levied in this section. However, only the property subject to tax under 02 AS 43.59.020 may be distrained. 03 Sec. 43.59.050. Termination of status; application. (a) The alternative 04 volumetric tax applicable to a natural gas project under AS 43.59.020 terminates on 05 (1) January 1, 2032, if commencement of construction of the first 730 06 miles of the gas pipeline related to the natural gas project has not begun by that date; 07 or 08 (2) January 1, 2037, if at least one major component of the natural gas 09 project has not been completed and commencement of commercial operations has not 10 occurred by that date. 11 (b) A natural gas project that does not meet the conditions of (a) of this section 12 and for which the alternative volumetric tax under AS 43.59.020 does not apply is 13 subject to all other state and municipal taxes on taxable property, including taxes 14 levied under AS 29.45.080 and AS 43.56.010. 15 (c) In this section, "commencement of construction" means the 16 (1) laying and welding together in an excavated trench multiple 17 sections of steel pipe that are intended for use as part of the gas pipeline; and 18 (2) establishment of at least one work camp along the gas pipeline 19 route that is intended to provide crew quarters and services during construction of the 20 gas pipeline. 21 Sec. 43.59.060. Reporting; regulations. (a) The owner of property subject to 22 tax under this section shall, at the request of the department, provide to the department 23 the information necessary to calculate the tax under this section, including capital 24 expenditures made by the owner. Notwithstanding AS 40.25.100(a) and AS 43.05.230, 25 the department shall hold confidential proprietary information provided to the 26 department under this subsection at the request of the owner. In this subsection 27 "proprietary information" means information that, if publicly disclosed, would 28 adversely affect the competitive position of the owner or materially diminish the 29 commercial value of the information to the owner. 30 (b) The department shall adopt regulations under AS 44.62 (Administrative 31 Procedure Act) to implement this chapter, including procedures for 01 (1) measuring throughput; 02 (2) throughput reporting; 03 (3) calculating the rolling average of throughput; and 04 (4) reporting and verifying capital expenditures for the purposes of the 05 calculations under AS 43.59.020(d) and adopting regulations under AS 43.59.030. 06 Sec. 43.59.100. Definitions. In this chapter, 07 (1) "capital expenditure" means an actual expenditure incurred to 08 acquire, construct, or improve a natural gas project or a component of a natural gas 09 project; 10 (2) "commencement of commercial operations" means the first flow of 11 natural gas through a natural gas project or a component of a natural gas project that 12 treats, transports, or processes a commercial amount of natural gas; 13 (3) "gas pipeline" 14 (A) means a main natural gas pipeline from the outlet flange of 15 the gas treatment plant on the North Slope to the inlet flange of the liquefied 16 natural gas plant located in the Kenai Peninsula region of the state; 17 (B) does not include any gas lines downstream of any offtake 18 point between a gas treatment plant and a liquefied natural gas plant; 19 (4) "gas treatment plant" means a facility and the related activities 20 required to receive natural gas from a Prudhoe Bay unit gas transmission line, a Point 21 Thomson unit gas transmission line, or other facilities, to treat the natural gas to 22 pipeline specifications, to dispose of or deliver byproducts, to deliver liquid products 23 for further transportation, and to deliver treated natural gas for transportation through a 24 gas pipeline; 25 (5) "liquefied natural gas plant" means a facility for liquefying natural 26 gas and includes structures, equipment, underlying land rights, and other associated 27 systems, storage, and facilities for off-loading liquefied natural gas; 28 (6) "natural gas project" and "project" means a natural gas project that 29 includes, collectively, a Prudhoe Bay unit gas transmission line, a Point Thomson unit 30 gas transmission line, a gas pipeline, a gas treatment plant, a liquefied natural gas 31 plant, and a marine terminal; in this paragraph, 01 (A) "marine terminal" means a terminal and those facilities 02 required to receive liquefied natural gas from the boundary of the liquefied 03 natural gas plant for marine transportation, including auxiliary vessels used in 04 the operation of the terminal; 05 (B) "Point Thomson unit gas transmission line" means a natural 06 gas transmission line from the outlet flange of the Point Thomson unit 07 production facility to the inlet flange of the gas treatment plant; and 08 (C) "Prudhoe Bay unit gas transmission line" means a natural 09 gas transmission line from the outlet flange of the Prudhoe Bay unit central gas 10 facility to the inlet flange of the gas treatment plant; 11 (7) "spur line" 12 (A) means 13 (i) a natural gas transmission or lateral line that 14 branches from the main gas pipeline for the primary purpose of 15 delivering natural gas to a local community or utility distribution 16 system; and 17 (ii) compressing and metering equipment and 18 interconnection facilities related to the transmission or lateral line 19 described in (A)(i) of this paragraph; 20 (B) does not include infrastructure used for the export of 21 natural gas or lateral lines not necessary for delivering natural gas to a local 22 community or utility distribution system; 23 (8) "throughput" 24 (A) means 25 (i) the volume of natural gas measured by summing all 26 volumes sold or otherwise delivered at each outlet or offtake point 27 along the gas pipeline; and 28 (ii) natural gas consumed as fuel for the operation of a 29 liquefaction facility; 30 (B) does not include natural gas consumed as fuel for pipeline 31 compression. 01 * Sec. 26.

AS 08.84 is amended by adding a new section to read: 27 Article 4A. Occupational Therapy Licensure Compact. 28 Sec. 08.84.189. Compact enacted. The Occupational Therapy Licensure 29 Compact as contained in this section is enacted into law and entered into on behalf of 30 the state with all other states and jurisdictions legally joining it in a form substantially 31 as follows: 01 SECTION 1. PURPOSE 02 The purpose of this Compact is to facilitate interstate practice of Occupational 03 Therapy with the goal of improving public access to Occupational Therapy services. 04 The Practice of Occupational Therapy occurs in the State where the patient/client is 05 located at the time of the patient/client encounter. The Compact preserves the 06 regulatory authority of States to protect public health and safety through the current 07 system of State licensure. 08 This Compact is designed to achieve the following objectives: 09 (A) Increase public access to Occupational Therapy services by providing for 10 the mutual recognition of other Member State licenses; 11 (B) Enhance the States' ability to protect the public's health and safety; 12 (C) Encourage the cooperation of Member States in regulating multi-State 13 Occupational Therapy Practice; 14 (D) Support spouses of relocating military members; 15 (E) Enhance the exchange of licensure, investigative, and disciplinary 16 information between Member States; 17 (F) Allow a Remote State to hold a provider of services with a Compact 18 Privilege in that State accountable to that State's practice standards; and 19 (G) Facilitate the use of Telehealth technology in order to increase access to 20 Occupational Therapy services. 21 SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS 22 As used in this Compact, and except as otherwise provided, the following 23 definitions shall apply: 24 (A) "Active Duty Military" means full-time duty status in the active 25 uniformed service of the United States, including members of the National Guard and 26 Reserve on active duty orders pursuant to 10 U.S.C. Chapter 1209 and 10 U.S.C. 27 Chapter 1211. 28 (B) "Adverse Action" means any administrative, civil, equitable, or criminal 29 action permitted by a State's laws which is imposed by a Licensing Board or other 30 authority against an Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant, 31 including actions against an individual's license or Compact Privilege such as censure, 01 revocation, suspension, probation, monitoring of the Licensee, or restriction on the 02 Licensee's practice. 03 (C) "Alternative Program" means a non-disciplinary monitoring process 04 approved by an Occupational Therapy Licensing Board. 05 (D) "Compact Privilege" means the authorization, which is equivalent to a 06 license, granted by a Remote State to allow a Licensee from another Member State to 07 practice as an Occupational Therapist or practice as an Occupational Therapy 08 Assistant in the Remote State under its laws and rules. The Practice of Occupational 09 Therapy occurs in the Member State where the patient/client is located at the time of 10 the patient/client encounter. 11 (E) "Continuing Competence/Education" means a requirement, as a condition 12 of license renewal, to provide evidence of participation in, and/or completion of, 13 educational and professional activities relevant to practice or area of work. 14 (F) "Current Significant Investigative Information" means Investigative 15 Information that a Licensing Board, after an inquiry or investigation that includes 16 notification and an opportunity for the Occupational Therapist or Occupational 17 Therapy Assistant to respond, if required by State law, has reason to believe is not 18 groundless and, if proved true, would indicate more than a minor infraction. 19 (G) "Data System" means a repository of information about Licensees, 20 including but not limited to license status, Investigative Information, Compact 21 Privileges, and Adverse Actions. 22 (H) "Encumbered License" means a license in which an Adverse Action 23 restricts the Practice of Occupational Therapy by the Licensee or said Adverse Action 24 has been reported to the National Practitioner Data Bank (NPDB). 25 (I) "Executive Committee" means a group of directors elected or appointed to 26 act on behalf of, and within the powers granted to them by, the Commission. 27 (J) "Home State" means the Member State that is the Licensee's Primary State 28 of Residence. 29 (K) "Impaired Practitioner" means individuals whose professional practice is 30 adversely affected by substance abuse, addiction, or other health-related conditions. 31 (L) "Investigative Information" means information, records, and/or documents 01 received or generated by an Occupational Therapy Licensing Board pursuant to an 02 investigation. 03 (M) "Jurisprudence Requirement" means the assessment of an individual's 04 knowledge of the laws and rules governing the Practice of Occupational Therapy in a 05 State. 06 (N) "Licensee" means an individual who currently holds an authorization from 07 the State to practice as an Occupational Therapist or as an Occupational Therapy 08 Assistant. 09 (O) "Member State" means a State that has enacted the Compact. 10 (P) "Occupational Therapist" means an individual who is licensed by a State 11 to practice Occupational Therapy. 12 (Q) "Occupational Therapy Assistant" means an individual who is licensed by 13 a State to assist in the Practice of Occupational Therapy. 14 (R) "Occupational Therapy," "Occupational Therapy Practice," and the 15 "Practice of Occupational Therapy" mean the care and services provided by an 16 Occupational Therapist or an Occupational Therapy Assistant as set forth in the 17 Member State's statutes and regulations. 18 (S) "Occupational Therapy Compact Commission" or "Commission" means 19 the national administrative body whose membership consists of all States that have 20 enacted the Compact. 21 (T) "Occupational Therapy Licensing Board" or "Licensing Board" means the 22 agency of a State that is authorized to license and regulate Occupational Therapists 23 and Occupational Therapy Assistants. 24 (U) "Primary State of Residence" means the state (also known as the Home 25 State) in which an Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant who is 26 not Active Duty Military declares a primary residence for legal purposes as verified 27 by: driver's license, federal income tax return, lease, deed, mortgage or voter 28 registration or other verifying documentation as further defined by Commission Rules. 29 (V) "Remote State" means a Member State other than the Home State, where a 30 Licensee is exercising or seeking to exercise the Compact Privilege. 31 (W) "Rule" means a regulation promulgated by the Commission that has the 01 force of law. 02 (X) "State" means any state, commonwealth, district, or territory of the United 03 States of America that regulates the Practice of Occupational Therapy. 04 (Y) "Single-State License" means an Occupational Therapist or Occupational 05 Therapy Assistant license issued by a Member State that authorizes practice only 06 within the issuing State and does not include a Compact Privilege in any other 07 Member State. 08 (Z) "Telehealth" means the application of telecommunication technology to 09 deliver Occupational Therapy services for assessment, intervention and/or 10 consultation. 11 SECTION 3. STATE PARTICIPATION IN THE COMPACT 12 (A) To participate in the Compact, a Member State shall: 13 (1) License Occupational Therapists and Occupational Therapy 14 Assistants; 15 (2) Participate fully in the Commission's Data System, including but 16 not limited to using the Commission's unique identifier as defined in Rules of the 17 Commission; 18 (3) Have a mechanism in place for receiving and investigating 19 complaints about Licensees; 20 (4) Notify the Commission, in compliance with the terms of the 21 Compact and Rules, of any Adverse Action or the availability of Investigative 22 Information regarding a Licensee; 23 (5) Implement or utilize procedures for considering the criminal 24 history records of applicants for an initial Compact Privilege. These procedures shall 25 include the submission of fingerprints or other biometric-based information by 26 applicants for the purpose of obtaining an applicant's criminal history record 27 information from the Federal Bureau of Investigation and the agency responsible for 28 retaining that State's criminal records; 29 (a) A Member State shall, within a time frame established by 30 the Commission, require a criminal background check for a Licensee 31 seeking/applying for a Compact Privilege whose Primary State of Residence is 01 that Member State, by receiving the results of the Federal Bureau of 02 Investigation criminal record search, and shall use the results in making 03 licensure decisions. 04 (b) Communication between a Member State, the Commission 05 and among Member States regarding the verification of eligibility for licensure 06 through the Compact shall not include any information received from the 07 Federal Bureau of Investigation relating to a federal criminal records check 08 performed by a Member State under Public Law 92-544. 09 (6) Comply with the Rules of the Commission; 10 (7) Utilize only a recognized national examination as a requirement for 11 licensure pursuant to the Rules of the Commission; and 12 (8) Have Continuing Competence/Education requirements as a 13 condition for license renewal. 14 (B) A Member State shall grant the Compact Privilege to a Licensee holding a 15 valid unencumbered license in another Member State in accordance with the terms of 16 the Compact and Rules. 17 (C) Member States may charge a fee for granting a Compact Privilege. 18 (D) A Member State shall provide for the State's delegate to attend all 19 Occupational Therapy Compact Commission meetings. 20 (E) Individuals not residing in a Member State shall continue to be able to 21 apply for a Member State's Single-State License as provided under the laws of each 22 Member State. However, the Single-State License granted to these individuals shall 23 not be recognized as granting the Compact Privilege in any other Member State. 24 (F) Nothing in this Compact shall affect the requirements established by a 25 Member State for the issuance of a Single-State License. 26 SECTION 4. COMPACT PRIVILEGE 27 (A) To exercise the Compact Privilege under the terms and provisions of the 28 Compact, the Licensee shall: 29 (1) Hold a license in the Home State; 30 (2) Have a valid United States Social Security Number or National 31 Practitioner Identification number; 01 (3) Have no encumbrance on any State license; 02 (4) Be eligible for a Compact Privilege in any Member State in 03 accordance with Section 4D, F, G, and H; 04 (5) Have paid all fines and completed all requirements resulting from 05 any Adverse Action against any license or Compact Privilege, and two years have 06 elapsed from the date of such completion; 07 (6) Notify the Commission that the Licensee is seeking the Compact 08 Privilege within a Remote State(s); 09 (7) Pay any applicable fees, including any State fee, for the Compact 10 Privilege; 11 (8) Complete a criminal background check in accordance with Section 12 3A(5); 13 (a) The Licensee shall be responsible for the payment of any 14 fee associated with the completion of a criminal background check. 15 (9) Meet any Jurisprudence Requirements established by the Remote 16 State(s) in which the Licensee is seeking a Compact Privilege; and 17 (10) Report to the Commission Adverse Action taken by any non- 18 Member State within 30 days from the date the Adverse Action is taken. 19 (B) The Compact Privilege is valid until the expiration date of the Home State 20 license. The Licensee must comply with the requirements of Section 4A to maintain 21 the Compact Privilege in the Remote State. 22 (C) A Licensee providing Occupational Therapy in a Remote State under the 23 Compact Privilege shall function within the laws and regulations of the Remote State. 24 (D) Occupational Therapy Assistants practicing in a Remote State shall be 25 supervised by an Occupational Therapist licensed or holding a Compact Privilege in 26 that Remote State. 27 (E) A Licensee providing Occupational Therapy in a Remote State is subject 28 to that State's regulatory authority. A Remote State may, in accordance with due 29 process and that State's laws, remove a Licensee's Compact Privilege in the Remote 30 State for a specific period of time, impose fines, and/or take any other necessary 31 actions to protect the health and safety of its citizens. The Licensee may be ineligible 01 for a Compact Privilege in any State until the specific time for removal has passed and 02 all fines are paid. 03 (F) If a Home State license is encumbered, the Licensee shall lose the 04 Compact Privilege in any Remote State until the following occur: 05 (1) The Home State license is no longer encumbered; and 06 (2) Two years have elapsed from the date on which the Home State 07 license is no longer encumbered in accordance with Section 4F(1). 08 (G) Once an Encumbered License in the Home State is restored to good 09 standing, the Licensee must meet the requirements of Section 4A to obtain a Compact 10 Privilege in any Remote State. 11 (H) If a Licensee's Compact Privilege in any Remote State is removed, the 12 individual may lose the Compact Privilege in any other Remote State until the 13 following occur: 14 (1) The specific period of time for which the Compact Privilege was 15 removed has ended; 16 (2) All fines have been paid and all conditions have been met; 17 (3) Two years have elapsed from the date of completing requirements 18 for Section 4H(1) and (2); and 19 (4) The Compact Privileges are reinstated by the Commission, and the 20 compact Data System is updated to reflect reinstatement. 21 (I) If a Licensee's Compact Privilege in any Remote State is removed due to 22 an erroneous charge, privileges shall be restored through the compact Data System. 23 (J) Once the requirements of Section 4H have been met, the Licensee must 24 meet the requirements in Section 4A to obtain a Compact Privilege in a Remote State. 25 SECTION 5. OBTAINING A NEW HOME STATE LICENSE BY VIRTUE OF 26 COMPACT PRIVILEGE 27 (A) An Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant may hold a 28 Home State license, which allows for Compact Privileges in Member States, in only 29 one Member State at a time. 30 (B) If an Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant changes 31 Primary State of Residence by moving between two Member States: 01 (1) The Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant 02 shall file an application for obtaining a new Home State license by virtue of a 03 Compact Privilege, pay all applicable fees, and notify the current and new Home State 04 in accordance with applicable Rules adopted by the Commission. 05 (2) Upon receipt of an application for obtaining a new Home State 06 license by virtue of compact privilege, the new Home State shall verify that the 07 Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant meets the pertinent criteria 08 outlined in Section 4 via the Data System, without need for primary source 09 verification except for: 10 (a) an FBI fingerprint based criminal background check if not 11 previously performed or updated pursuant to applicable Rules adopted by the 12 Commission in accordance with Public Law 92-544; 13 (b) other criminal background check as required by the new 14 Home State; and 15 (c) submission of any requisite Jurisprudence Requirements of 16 the new Home State. 17 (3) The former Home State shall convert the former Home State 18 license into a Compact Privilege once the new Home State has activated the new 19 Home State license in accordance with applicable Rules adopted by the Commission. 20 (4) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Compact, if the 21 Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant cannot meet the criteria in 22 Section 4, the new Home State shall apply its requirements for issuing a new Single- 23 State License. 24 (5) The Occupational Therapist or the Occupational Therapy Assistant 25 shall pay all applicable fees to the new Home State in order to be issued a new Home 26 State license. 27 (C) If an Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant changes 28 Primary State of Residence by moving from a Member State to a non-Member State, 29 or from a non-Member State to a Member State, the State criteria shall apply for 30 issuance of a Single-State License in the new State. 31 (D) Nothing in this compact shall interfere with a Licensee's ability to hold a 01 Single-State License in multiple States; however, for the purposes of this compact, a 02 Licensee shall have only one Home State license. 03 (E) Nothing in this Compact shall affect the requirements established by a 04 Member State for the issuance of a Single-State License. 05 SECTION 6. ADVERSE ACTIONS 13 (A) A Home State shall have exclusive power to impose Adverse Action 14 against an Occupational Therapist's or Occupational Therapy Assistant's license issued 15 by the Home State. 16 (B) In addition to the other powers conferred by State law, a Remote State 17 shall have the authority, in accordance with existing State due process law, to: 18 (1) Take Adverse Action against an Occupational Therapist's or 19 Occupational Therapy Assistant's Compact Privilege within that Member State. 20 (2) Issue subpoenas for both hearings and investigations that require 21 the attendance and testimony of witnesses as well as the production of evidence. 22 Subpoenas issued by a Licensing Board in a Member State for the attendance and 23 testimony of witnesses or the production of evidence from another Member State shall 24 be enforced in the latter State by any court of competent jurisdiction, according to the 25 practice and procedure of that court applicable to subpoenas issued in proceedings 26 pending before it. The issuing authority shall pay any witness fees, travel expenses, 27 mileage and other fees required by the service statutes of the State in which the 28 witnesses or evidence are located. 29 (C) For purposes of taking Adverse Action, the Home State shall give the 30 same priority and effect to reported conduct received from a Member State as it would 31 if the conduct had occurred within the Home State. In so doing, the Home State shall 01 apply its own State laws to determine appropriate action. 02 (D) The Home State shall complete any pending investigations of an 03 Occupational Therapist or Occupational Therapy Assistant who changes Primary State 04 of Residence during the course of the investigations. Failure to provide service of process to the Commission shall render a 23 judgment or order void as to the Commission, this Compact, or promulgated Rules. 24 (B) Default, Technical Assistance, and Termination 25 (1) If the Commission determines that a Member State has defaulted in 26 the performance of its obligations or responsibilities under this Compact or the 27 promulgated Rules, the Commission shall: 28 (a) Provide written notice to the defaulting State and other 29 Member States of the nature of the default, the proposed means of curing the 30 default and/or any other action to be taken by the Commission; and 31 (b) Provide remedial training and specific technical assistance 01 regarding the default. 02 (2) If a State in default fails to cure the default, the defaulting State 03 may be terminated from the Compact upon an affirmative vote of a majority of the 04 Member States, and all rights, privileges and benefits conferred by this Compact may 05 be terminated on the effective date of termination. A cure of the default does not 06 relieve the offending State of obligations or liabilities incurred during the period of 07 default. 08 (3) Termination of membership in the Compact shall be imposed only 09 after all other means of securing compliance have been exhausted. Notice of intent to 10 suspend or terminate shall be given by the Commission to the governor, the majority 11 and minority leaders of the defaulting State's legislature, and each of the Member 12 States. 13 (4) A State that has been terminated is responsible for all assessments, 14 obligations, and liabilities incurred through the effective date of termination, including 15 obligations that extend beyond the effective date of termination. 16 (5) The Commission shall not bear any costs related to a State that is 17 found to be in default or that has been terminated from the Compact, unless agreed 18 upon in writing between the Commission and the defaulting State. 19 (6) The defaulting State may appeal the action of the Commission by 20 petitioning the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia or the federal district 21 where the Commission has its principal offices. The prevailing member shall be 22 awarded all costs of such litigation, including reasonable attorney's fees. 23 (C) Dispute Resolution 24 (1) Upon request by a Member State, the Commission shall attempt to 25 resolve disputes related to the Compact that arise among Member States and between 26 member and non-Member States. 27 (2) The Commission shall promulgate a Rule providing for both 28 mediation and binding dispute resolution for disputes as appropriate. 29 (D) Enforcement 30 (1) The Commission, in the reasonable exercise of its discretion, shall 31 enforce the provisions and Rules of this Compact. 01 (2) By majority vote, the Commission may initiate legal action in the 02 United States District Court for the District of Columbia or the federal district where 03 the Commission has its principal offices against a Member State in default to enforce 04 compliance with the provisions of the Compact and its promulgated Rules and bylaws. 05 The relief sought may include both injunctive relief and damages. In the event judicial 06 enforcement is necessary, the prevailing member shall be awarded all costs of such 07 litigation, including reasonable attorney's fees. 08 (3) The remedies herein shall not be the exclusive remedies of the 09 Commission. The Commission may pursue any other remedies available under federal 10 or State law. 11 SECTION 12. The provisions, which become 16 effective at that time, shall be limited to the powers granted to the Commission 17 relating to assembly and the promulgation of Rules. Thereafter, the Commission shall 18 meet and exercise Rulemaking powers necessary to the implementation and 19 administration of the Compact. 20 (B) Any State that joins the Compact subsequent to the Commission's initial 21 adoption of the Rules shall be subject to the Rules as they exist on the date on which 22 the Compact becomes law in that State. Any Rule that has been previously adopted by 23 the Commission shall have the full force and effect of law on the day the Compact 24 becomes law in that State. 25 (C) Any Member State may withdraw from this Compact by enacting a statute 26 repealing the same. 27 (1) A Member State's withdrawal shall not take effect until six (6) 28 months after enactment of the repealing statute. 29 (2) Withdrawal shall not affect the continuing requirement of the 30 withdrawing State's Occupational Therapy Licensing Board to comply with the 31 investigative and Adverse Action reporting requirements of this act prior to the 01 effective date of withdrawal. 02 (D) Nothing contained in this Compact shall be construed to invalidate or 03 prevent any Occupational Therapy licensure agreement or other cooperative 04 arrangement between a Member State and a non-Member State that does not conflict 05 with the provisions of this Compact. 06 (E) This Compact may be amended by the Member States. No amendment to 07 this Compact shall become effective and binding upon any Member State until it is 08 enacted into the laws of all Member States. 09 SECTION 13. CONSTRUCTION AND SEVERABILITY 10 This Compact shall be liberally construed so as to effectuate the purposes 11 thereof. The provisions of this Compact shall be severable and if any phrase, clause, 12 sentence or provision of this Compact is declared to be contrary to the constitution of 13 any Member State or of the United States or the applicability thereof to any 14 government, agency, person, or circumstance is held invalid, the validity of the 15 remainder of this Compact and the applicability thereof to any government, agency, 16 person, or circumstance shall not be affected thereby. If this Compact shall be held 17 contrary to the constitution of any Member State, the Compact shall remain in full 18 force and effect as to the remaining Member States and in full force and effect as to 19 the Member State affected as to all severable matters. 20 SECTION 14.

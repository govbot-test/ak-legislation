HB 362: "An Act relating to the practice of respiratory care; relating to the duties of the Department of Commerce, Community, and Economic Development; relating to telehealth provided by respiratory care practitioners; relating to medical malpractice liability of respiratory care practitioners; relating to immunity from civil liability for a respiratory care practitioner providing free health care services; relating to respiratory care practitioners providing documentation and treatment plans for public school students with asthma or anaphylaxis; prohibiting unfair discrimination under group health insurance against respiratory care practitioners; and providing for an effective date." 

00                            HOUSE BILL NO. 362                                                                          
01 "An Act relating to the practice of respiratory care; relating to the duties of the                                     
02 Department of Commerce, Community, and Economic Development; relating to                                                
03 telehealth provided by respiratory care practitioners; relating to medical malpractice                                  
04 liability of respiratory care practitioners; relating to immunity from civil liability for a                            
05 respiratory care practitioner providing free health care services; relating to respiratory                              
06 care practitioners providing documentation and treatment plans for public school

16 Sec. 08.90.010. License required. Unless licensed under this chapter, a person                                        
17       may not                                                                                                           
18                 (1)  practice respiratory care; or                                                                      
19 (2)  represent that the person is a respiratory care practitioner, including                                            
20 using the titles "licensed respiratory care practitioner" or "R.C.P." or any other words,                               
21       abbreviations, signs, or symbols indicating that the person is a licensee.                                        
22 Sec. 08.90.020. Licensure. (a) The department shall issue a license to practice                                       
23 respiratory care to an applicant who submits to the department written evidence,                                        
24       verified by oath, that the applicant has                                                                          
25                 (1)  obtained a high school diploma or its equivalent;                                                  
26 (2)  successfully completed an accredited respiratory care educational                                                  
27       program;                                                                                                          
28 (3)  passed an examination for a registered respiratory therapist                                                       
29 credential that is administered by a nationally recognized organization for respiratory                                 
30       therapists and approved by the department in regulation;                                                          
31                 (4)  paid the required fees; and                                                                        



01 (5)  met all other requirements established by the department in                                                        
02       regulation.                                                                                                       
03 (b)  The department shall issue a license to practice respiratory care to an                                            
04 applicant who submits to the department, before the date that is one year after the                                     
05 effective date of this Act, the required fees and written evidence, verified by oath, that                              
06 the applicant was employed in the state to practice respiratory care under the direction                                
07 of a physician on or before the effective date of this Act. An applicant issued a license                               
08 under this subsection may renew the license without meeting the initial licensure                                       
09 requirements provided under (a)(2) and (3) of this section, but is otherwise subject to                                 
10       this chapter.                                                                                                     
11 Sec. 08.90.030. Licensure by credentials. The department shall issue a license                                        
12 by credentials to practice respiratory care to an applicant who submits to the                                          
13       department written evidence, verified by oath, that the applicant                                                 
14 (1)  is currently licensed to practice respiratory care in another state, a                                             
15 territory of the United States, or a foreign country with requirements for licensure that                               
16 the department determines are substantially equivalent to those required under this                                     
17       chapter;                                                                                                          
18 (2)  has current valid credentials as a registered respiratory therapist                                                
19 conferred by a nationally recognized organization for respiratory therapists that is                                    
20       approved by the department in regulation;                                                                         
21                 (3)  paid the required fees; and                                                                        
22 (4)  met all other requirements established by the department in                                                        
23       regulation.                                                                                                       
24 Sec. 08.90.040. Evidence of licensure in other jurisdictions. An applicant for                                        
25 a license under AS 08.90.020 or 08.90.030 must submit evidence to the department of                                     
26 all licenses granted to the applicant to practice respiratory care in another state, a                                  
27 territory of the United States, or a foreign country and whether that license is in good                                
28       standing.                                                                                                         
29 Sec. 08.90.050. License renewal. The department may renew a license issued                                            
30 under this chapter only if the licensee submits proof to the department that the licensee                               
31 completed 20 credit hours of continuing respiratory care education during the previous                                  



01       licensing period.                                                                                                 
02 Sec. 08.90.060. Scope of practice of respiratory care; qualified medical                                              
03 directors. (a) A licensee may, under the supervision of a qualified medical director                                  
04 and as authorized either under a medically approved protocol or under a written or oral                                 
05 order from an authorized provider, perform the management, rehabilitation, diagnostic                                   
06 evaluation, and care of a patient with a deficiency or abnormality of the                                               
07       cardiopulmonary system and associated aspects of other system functions, including                                
08                 (1)  providing pulmonary care services that are safe, aseptic, and                                      
09       preventive or restorative to the patient;                                                                         
10 (2)  administering to the patient pharmacological, diagnostic, and                                                      
11 therapeutic agents related to respiratory care procedures that are necessary to                                         
12 implement a treatment, disease prevention, pulmonary rehabilitative, or diagnostic                                      
13       regimen prescribed by an authorized provider;                                                                     
14 (3)  making observations of the patient and monitoring the patient's                                                    
15 signs, symptoms, behavior, and physical responses to respiratory therapy treatment to                                   
16 determine whether the patient exhibits any abnormal characteristics and, based on                                       
17 those determinations, refer the patient, implement respiratory therapy protocols, or                                    
18       change the patient's treatment regimen;                                                                           
19                 (4)  employing on the patient the diagnostic or therapeutic use of                                      
20                      (A)  medical gases, excluding general anesthesia;                                                  
21                      (B)  aerosols;                                                                                     
22                      (C)  humidification;                                                                               
23 (D)  pharmacological agents related to respiratory care                                                                 
24            procedures;                                                                                                  
25                      (E)  mechanical or physiological ventilatory support;                                              
26                      (F)  bronchopulmonary hygiene procedures;                                                          
27                      (G)  oxygen therapy;                                                                               
28                      (H)  telehealth as permitted under AS 08.02.130;                                                   
29                      (I)  cardiopulmonary disease management; and                                                       
30                      (J)  instruction on tobacco cessation;                                                             
31 (5)  diagnostic or therapeutic techniques that may be medically                                                         



01       essential to maintaining the patient's good respiratory health, including                                         
02                      (A)  maintaining natural airways;                                                                  
03                      (B)  inserting, without cutting tissue, and maintaining artificial                                 
04            airways;                                                                                                     
05                      (C)  collecting blood and other body fluids, including specimens                                   
06            from the respiratory tract;                                                                                  
07                      (D)  collecting inspired and expired gas samples;                                                  
08                      (E)  analyzing blood samples and respiratory secretions;                                           
09                      (F)  measuring ventilatory volumes, pressures, and flows;                                          
10 (G)  measuring hemodynamic and other physiological                                                                      
11            parameters of the cardiopulmonary system;                                                                    
12                      (H)  performing cardiopulmonary resuscitation; and                                                 
13 (I)  implementing and maintaining environmental control                                                                 
14            systems and hyperbaric therapy;                                                                              
15 (6)  transcribing the written or oral orders of an authorized provider                                                  
16       regarding the patient;                                                                                            
17 (7)  providing instruction and education to the patient on the proper                                                   
18 methods of providing self-care and preventing cardiopulmonary disease and other                                         
19       conditions requiring the use of respiratory care equipment and techniques;                                        
20                 (8)  participating in air and ground transport of the patient;                                          
21                 (9)  inserting and maintaining invasive lines for the patient;                                          
22 (10)  managing and maintaining an extracorporeal membrane                                                               
23       oxygenation unit for the patient;                                                                                 
24 (11)  determining which medically approved respiratory care protocols                                                   
25 should be followed for the patient, in the absence of an authorized provider, during an                                 
26       emergency;                                                                                                        
27 (12)  establishing procedures for administering treatment and testing of                                                
28 the patient ordered by an authorized provider to aid diagnosis, patient health                                          
29       monitoring, or medical research; and                                                                              
30                 (13)  providing inhalation and respiratory therapy to the patient.                                      
31 (b)  A licensee may practice respiratory care in a clinic, hospital, skilled                                            



01 nursing facility, or private dwelling. A licensee may practice respiratory care in                                      
02 another location if the department determines that the location is appropriate or                                       
03 necessary, the location is authorized by a physician in a written or oral order, and the                                
04 practices performed at the location are performed under the supervision of a qualified                                  
05       medical director.                                                                                                 
06            (c)  A licensee may perform advanced techniques not ordinarily in the scope of                               
07       practice of respiratory care if the licensee has received formal training to perform                              
08       those techniques from a respiratory care educational program that                                                 
09                 (1)  is accredited, supervised, and structured;                                                         
10 (2)  evaluated the competence of the licensee through a standardized                                                    
11       testing mechanism; and                                                                                            
12                 (3)  included clinical, didactic, and laboratory activities.                                            
13 (d)  A qualified medical director supervising a licensee under (a) of this section                                      
14 shall be readily accessible to the licensee and is responsible for the quality, safety, and                             
15       appropriateness of the respiratory care services provided by the licensee.                                        
16            (e)  In this section,                                                                                        
17 (1)  "authorized provider" means a physician or physician assistant                                                     
18 licensed under AS 08.64 or advanced practice registered nurse licensed under                                            
19       AS 08.68 acting within the scope of the license;                                                                  
20 (2)  "home health agency" means a public agency or private                                                              
21 organization, or a subdivision of a public agency or private organization, that                                         
22 primarily engages in providing skilled nursing services in combination with                                             
23 respiratory therapy, physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, or services                                
24 provided by a home health aide to an individual in the individual's home, an assisted                                   
25       living home, or another residential setting;                                                                      
26 (3)  "public agency" means an agency operated by the state or a local                                                   
27       government;                                                                                                       
28                 (4)  "qualified medical director" means a physician who                                                 
29 (A)  is licensed to practice medicine in the state and is legally                                                       
30 authorized to direct an inpatient or outpatient health care organization, home                                          
31 health agency, assisted living home, or the health care division or department                                          



01            of a hospital, medical clinic, or other medical health maintenance facility; and                             
02                      (B)  has substantial professional training or experience in the                                    
03            management of acute and chronic respiratory disorders;                                                       
04 (5)  "subdivision" means a component of a multi-function facility or                                                    
05 home health agency, such as the home health care division of a hospital or the division                                 
06 of a public agency, that independently meets the requirements for licensure as a home                                   
07       health agency.                                                                                                    
08            Sec. 08.90.070. Disciplinary sanctions. (a) The department may revoke,                                     
09       suspend, or refuse to issue a license to a person if the department finds that the person                         
10 (1)  secured or attempted to secure a license to practice respiratory care                                              
11       through fraud, deceit, or intentional misrepresentation;                                                          
12                 (2)  is unfit or incompetent to practice respiratory care;                                              
13 (3)  uses drugs or alcohol in a manner that affects the person's ability to                                             
14       practice respiratory care competently and safely;                                                                 
15 (4)  engaged in dishonest or unethical conduct in connection with the                                                   
16       delivery of respiratory care services to patients;                                                                
17 (5)  used or attempted to use as valid a license to practice respiratory                                                
18       care that was illegally or fraudulently obtained;                                                                 
19 (6)  practiced respiratory care after the revocation, suspension, or                                                    
20       expiration of the person's license;                                                                               
21 (7)  failed to meet the standards of practice for respiratory care,                                                     
22       including                                                                                                         
23 (A)  wilfully and repeatedly ordering and performing laboratory                                                         
24            tests or studies that were unnecessary and clinically unjustifiable; or                                      
25 (B)  administrating treatment that was unnecessary and                                                                  
26            clinically unjustifiable;                                                                                    
27 (8)  violated, or aided or abetted another person in violating, this                                                    
28       chapter;                                                                                                          
29                 (9)  has been convicted of                                                                              
30 (A)  a felony that materially affects the ability to safely practice                                                    
31            respiratory care; or                                                                                         



01                      (B)  insurance fraud;                                                                              
02                 (10)  failed to disclose that a license to practice respiratory care in                                 
03       another state, a territory of the United States, or a foreign country is not in good                              
04       standing.                                                                                                         
05 (b)  When determining whether to refuse to issue a license to an applicant or                                           
06 impose a disciplinary sanction on a licensee under (a)(3) of this section, the                                          
07 department shall consider whether an applicant or licensee is participating in a                                        
08       substance abuse program.                                                                                          
09            Sec. 08.90.080. Exceptions. (a) This chapter does not apply to                                             
10 (1)  a person licensed in this state under a statute outside this chapter                                               
11 who performs respiratory care within the scope of practice for which the person's                                       
12       license was issued;                                                                                               
13 (2)  a student enrolled in an accredited respiratory care educational                                                   
14       program who is                                                                                                    
15 (A)  practicing respiratory care as an integral part of the                                                             
16            program; or                                                                                                  
17 (B)  practicing respiratory care, within limits established by the                                                      
18 department, as an employee of a health care provider while a licensee or                                                
19 physician is available to communicate with, consult with, and assist the                                                
20            student;                                                                                                     
21 (3)  a person who is not a licensee and practices respiratory care as self-                                             
22       care or care for a member of the person's family;                                                                 
23                 (4)  a person practicing respiratory care in an emergency;                                              
24 (5)  a person practicing respiratory care within the scope of the person's                                              
25       duties while                                                                                                      
26                      (A)  in the armed forces of the United States; or                                                  
27                      (B)  working in a federal facility;                                                                
28 (6)  a person employed in a diagnostic laboratory, physician's office,                                                  
29 clinic, or outpatient treatment facility that is administering treatment or performing                                  
30       diagnostic procedures in that location under the direction of a physician; and                                    
31 (7)  a person employed by a durable or home medical equipment                                                           



01       company who delivers, calibrates, or maintains respiratory care equipment and does                                
02       not assess or evaluate patients.                                                                                  
03            (b)  A student practicing respiratory care shall use the title                                               
04                 (1)  "student respiratory care practitioner" or "student R.C.P." if                                     
05       practicing under (a)(2)(A) of this section; and                                                                   
06                 (2)  "student respiratory care practitioner," "trainee respiratory care                                 
07       practitioner," "student R.C.P.," or "trainee R.C.P." if practicing under (a)(2)(B) of this                        
08       section.                                                                                                          
09            Sec. 08.90.090. Criminal penalty. A person who violates or aids a person in a                              
10       violation of this chapter is guilty of a class B misdemeanor.                                                     
11 Sec. 08.90.100. Regulations. The department may adopt regulations to carry                                            
12       out the purposes of this chapter.                                                                                 
13            Sec. 08.90.990. Definitions. In this chapter,                                                              
14 (1)  "accredited respiratory care educational program" means a                                                          
15       respiratory care educational program that is                                                                      
16 (A)  accredited by a national health education accrediting body

18                      (B)  approved by the department;

20       and Economic Development;                                                                                         
21 (3)  "licensee" means a person licensed to practice respiratory care                                                    
22       under this chapter;                                                                                               
23 (4)  "physician" means a person licensed as a physician under                                                           
24       AS 08.64.

and 07 WHEREAS, in 1910, the seal was redesigned, and the new seal was retained through 08 Alaska's territorial days up to statehood, when it became the official state seal; and 09 WHEREAS the state seal reflects Alaska at one point in time and places significant 10 emphasis on western exploration, resource extraction, and agricultural activity while 11 excluding other values held by many in the state today; and 12 WHEREAS review and reconsideration of the state seal to adequately reflect the 13 state's original inhabitants, Alaska Native cultures, and contemporary themes serves an 14 important public purpose; and 15 WHEREAS it is timely and appropriate to reimagine the symbol that represents the 16 state values of justice and perseverance in a manner that honors the shared heritage of the 01 people of the state and respects the Native people of Alaska; 02 BE IT RESOLVED by the Alaska State Legislature that the Legislative Commission 03 to Review the State Seal of Alaska is established as a joint commission of the Alaska State 04 Legislature for the purposes of 05 (1) evaluating the current official seal of the state, including those features that 06 may be harmful to or misunderstood by residents of the state and citizens of the world; 07 (2) recommending changes to the official seal of the state, including preparing 08 draft legislation necessary to carry out the recommendations of the commission, so that the 09 official seal might better represent the historical and contemporary commitment of the people 10 of the state to peace, justice, liberty, and equality; 11 (3) making recommendations to the legislature relating to the design and 12 content of an educational program concerned with the history and meaning of the official seal 13 of the state; 14 (4) submitting recommendations and accompanying materials to the 15 legislature in a report to the senate secretary and the chief clerk of the house of representatives 16 on or before May 14, 2027, along with a summary of the commission's work, findings, and 17 recommendations, and notifying the members of the legislature that the report is available; 18 and 19 (5) making its report available to the public on the legislature's Internet 20 website; and be it 21 FURTHER RESOLVED that the commission consists of 14 members who, except 22 for those set out in (1) - (3) of this clause, shall be appointed jointly on the basis of their 23 cultural and historical expertise by the president of the senate and the speaker of the house of 24 representatives as follows: 25 (1) three members of the house of representatives appointed by the speaker of 26 the house of representatives, including one member of the minority organizational caucus; 27 (2) three members of the senate appointed by the president of the senate, 28 including one member of the minority organizational caucus; 29 (3) the lieutenant governor or the lieutenant governor's designee; 30 (4) one member representing the Alaska State Council on the Arts; 31 (5) one member representing the Alaska Humanities Forum; 01 (6) one member representing the Alaska Historical Society; 02 (7) one member representing the University of Alaska; 03 (8) one member representing the Rasmuson Foundation; 04 (9) one member representing the First Alaskans Institute; 05 (10) one student enrolled in good standing at a public high school who will be 06 in the student's senior year on the date of the first meeting of the commission; and be it 07 FURTHER RESOLVED that 08 (1) the president of the senate and the speaker of the house of representatives 09 shall jointly appoint the chair of the commission from among the legislative members of the 10 commission; 11 (2) commission members shall be appointed not later than 30 days after the 12 effective date of this resolution; 13 (3) the commission shall meet at the call of the chair; 14 (4) a majority of the commission constitutes a quorum; 15 (5) the commission may meet and vote telephonically and by videoconference; 16 (6) a vacancy on the commission shall be filled in the same manner as the 17 original selection or appointment; 18 (7) the commission may request data and other information from state and 19 legislative agencies; and be it 20 FURTHER RESOLVED that the staff of the legislator who chairs the commission 21 shall provide administrative and other support to the commission; and be it 22 FURTHER RESOLVED that, to fund its work, the commission 23 (1) shall seek, accept, and use grants or gifts of money and professional, 24 consulting, clerical, or other services and supplies from the federal government and other 25 public and private sources; and 26 (2) may accept appropriations from the legislature; and be it 27 FURTHER RESOLVED that members of the commission serve without 28 compensation but are entitled to per diem and travel expenses; and be it 29 FURTHER RESOLVED that the commission expires on May 14, 2027, unless two- 30 thirds of the members vote that an extension is necessary to complete the work of the 31 commission, in which case, the commission expires upon completion of its work or on 01 June 30, 2027, whichever occurs first.

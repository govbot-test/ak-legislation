and 06 WHEREAS idiopathic inflammatory myopathies, collectively referred to as myositis, 07 are rare, chronic autoimmune muscle-wasting diseases, can cause debilitating muscle 08 inflammation, pain, fatigue, and trouble swallowing, and can result in myositis-associated 09 interstitial lung disease; and 10 WHEREAS more research is needed to identify the causes of myositis and modes of 11 treatment for the myositis group of diseases, including anti-melanoma differentiation- 12 associated gene 5 dermatomyositis, antisynthetase syndrome, dermatomyositis, juvenile 13 myositis, necrotizing autoimmune myopathy, inclusion body myositis, and polymyositis; and 14 WHEREAS, because myositis is difficult to diagnose, treatment is often delayed, and 15 individuals who have myositis often experience difficulty finding a health care provider with 16 expertise in the disease; and 17 WHEREAS individuals who suffer from myositis experience a reduced quality of life 01 and a shortened life expectancy from complications related to myositis, especially those with 02 inclusion body myositis, and no cure has been found; and 03 WHEREAS The Myositis Association is the leading international patient advocacy 04 organization serving the myositis community, with a focus on patient services, education, 05 research, and public awareness to achieve a world without myositis; and 06 WHEREAS The Myositis Association is organizing a nationwide observance of 07 myositis awareness for the month of May 2026; 08 BE IT RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature encourages residents of the 09 state to come together, learn about the symptoms and types of myositis, and support 10 individuals and families in local communities who are affected by myositis and can benefit 11 from greater awareness of these rare diseases; and be it 12 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature proclaims the month of 13 May 2026 as Myositis Awareness Month.

relating to collaborative agreements between physicians and physician assistants; relating to the practice of medicine; relating to health care providers; and relating to provisions regarding physician assistants in contracts between certain health care providers and health care insurers." 00 HOUSE CS FOR CS FOR SENATE BILL NO. 89(HSS) 01 "An Act relating to physician assistants; relating to collaborative agreements between 02 physicians and physician assistants; relating to the practice of medicine; relating to 03 health care providers; and relating to provisions regarding physician assistants in 04 contracts between certain health care providers and health care insurers." 05 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA: 06 * Section 1. AS 08.64.010 is amended to read: 07 Sec. 08.64.010. Creation and membership of State Medical Board. The 08 governor shall appoint a board of medical examiners, to be known as the State 09 Medical Board, consisting of five physicians licensed in the state and residing in as 10 many separate geographical areas of the state as possible, one physician assistant 11 licensed in the state [UNDER AS 08.64.107], and two persons with no direct 12 financial interest in the health care industry. 13 * Sec. 2. AS 08.64.107 is repealed and reenacted to read: 14 Sec. 08.64.107. Scope of practice of physician assistants; collaborative 01 agreements. (a) A physician assistant may provide any medical service for which the 02 physician assistant is qualified to provide by education, training, and experience and 03 competent to perform, including 04 (1) obtaining a patient's comprehensive health history and performing 05 a physical examination; 06 (2) evaluating, diagnosing, managing, and providing medical 07 treatment; 08 (3) ordering and evaluating a diagnostic study or therapeutic 09 procedure; 10 (4) performing limited sonography on a focused imaging target to 11 assess specific and limited information about a patient's medical condition or to 12 provide real-time visual guidance for another procedure; 13 (5) educating a patient on health promotion and disease prevention; 14 (6) providing medical consultation to patients or other health care 15 providers; 16 (7) writing a medical order; 17 (8) obtaining informed consent; 18 (9) supervising, delegating, and assigning therapeutic and diagnostic 19 measures to licensed or unlicensed personnel in accordance with regulations adopted 20 under AS 08.64.106; 21 (10) certifying the health or disability of a patient as required by a 22 local, state, or federal program; 23 (11) authenticating a document with the signature, certification, stamp, 24 verification, affidavit, or endorsement of the physician assistant if the document may 25 be authenticated by the signature, certification, stamp, verification, affidavit, or 26 endorsement of a physician; 27 (12) pronouncing death in accordance with AS 18.08.089; 28 (13) planning and initiating a therapeutic regimen that includes 29 ordering and prescribing nonpharmacological interventions; in this paragraph, 30 "nonpharmacological intervention" includes durable medical equipment, nutrition, 31 blood and blood products, diagnostic support services, home health care, hospice, and 01 physical and occupational therapy; 02 (14) prescribing schedule II, III, IV, and V controlled substances under 03 federal law and all federal legend drugs; 04 (15) administering and procuring drugs and medical devices; 05 (16) dispensing a drug if pharmacy services are not reasonably 06 available and dispensing the drug is in the best interest of the patient or an emergency 07 exists; and 08 (17) requesting, receiving, and signing for a professional sample and 09 distributing a professional sample to a patient; in this paragraph, "professional sample" 10 means a unit of a prescription drug that is not for sale and given at no cost to the 11 physician assistant to promote the drug's use. 12 (b) A physician assistant is responsible for the care provided to a patient by 13 the physician assistant. 14 (c) Except as provided in (d) of this section, a physician assistant may only 15 practice in the state under a written collaborative agreement entered into with a 16 physician licensed under this chapter or a physician exempt from licensing under 17 AS 08.64.370. The collaborative agreement must describe how the collaboration will 18 occur between the physician assistant and the physician. A physician assistant who is 19 practicing under a collaborative agreement shall provide to the board with the 20 physician assistant's application for licensure or renewal a written attestation that the 21 physician assistant is practicing under a collaborative agreement. The physician 22 assistant shall provide a copy of the written collaborative agreement to the board upon 23 request. 24 (d) A physician assistant may practice without a collaborative agreement if 25 (1) the physician assistant is practicing at 26 (A) a facility directed by a licensed physician, osteopath, or 27 podiatrist; 28 (B) a health care facility licensed by the Department of Health 29 under AS 47.32; 30 (C) a health care facility that is operated by the federal 31 government or a tribal organization as defined in 25 U.S.C. 450b; 01 (D) a federally qualified health center or rural health clinic; or 02 (E) a military and veterans' facility; or 03 (2) the board has, upon application of the physician assistant, 04 authorized the physician assistant to practice at a facility not described in (1) of this 05 subsection without obtaining a collaborative agreement. 06 * Sec. 3. AS 08.64 is amended by adding a new section to read: 07 Sec. 08.64.206. Qualifications for physician assistant applicants. Each 08 physician assistant applicant shall meet the qualifications prescribed in 09 AS 08.64.200(a)(3) - (5) and shall submit 10 (1) a certificate of graduation obtained from a physician assistant 11 program accredited, at the time of graduation, by 12 (A) the American Medical Association's Committee on Allied 13 Health Education and Accreditation or the Commission on Accreditation of 14 Allied Health Education Programs if the applicant graduated before January 1, 15 2001; or 16 (B) the Accreditation Review Commission on Education for the 17 Physician Assistant if the applicant graduated on or after January 1, 2001; 18 (2) proof of current certification issued by the National Commission on 19 Certification of Physician Assistants; and 20 (3) proof of receiving a passing score on the physician assistant 21 national certifying examination offered by the National Commission on Certification 22 of Physician Assistants. 23 * Sec. 4. AS 08.64.230 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 24 (d) If a physician assistant applicant passes the examination and meets the 25 requirements of AS 08.64.206 and 08.64.255, the board or its executive secretary shall 26 grant a license to the applicant to practice the acts permitted under AS 08.64.107. 27 * Sec. 5. AS 08.64.250(a) is amended to read: 28 (a) The board may waive the examination requirement and license by 29 credentials if the physician, osteopath, physician assistant, or podiatry applicant 30 meets the requirements of AS 08.64.200, 08.64.205, 08.64.206, or 08.64.209, submits 31 proof of continued competence as required by regulation, pays the required fee, and 01 has 02 (1) an active license from a board of medical examiners established 03 under the laws of a state or territory of the United States or a province or territory of 04 Canada issued after thorough examination; or 05 (2) passed an examination as specified by the board in regulations. 06 * Sec. 6. AS 08.64.270(a) is amended to read: 07 (a) The board, a member of the board, the executive secretary, or a person 08 designated by the board to issue temporary permits may issue a temporary permit to 09 an [A PHYSICIAN APPLICANT, OSTEOPATH APPLICANT, OR PODIATRY] 10 applicant who meets the requirements of AS 08.64.200, 08.64.205, 08.64.206, 11 08.64.209, or 08.64.225 and pays the required fee. 12 * Sec. 7. AS 08.64.275(a) is amended to read: 13 (a) A member of the board, its executive secretary, or a person designated by 14 the board to issue temporary permits may grant a temporary permit to a physician, 15 [OR] osteopath, or physician assistant for the purpose of 16 (1) substituting for another physician, [OR] osteopath, or physician 17 assistant licensed in this state; 18 (2) being temporarily employed by a physician, [OR] osteopath, or 19 physician assistant licensed in this state while that physician, [OR] osteopath, or 20 physician assistant evaluates the permittee for permanent employment; or 21 (3) being temporarily employed by a hospital or community mental 22 health center while the facility attempts to fill a vacant permanent physician, [OR] 23 osteopath, or physician assistant staff position with a physician, [OR] osteopath, or 24 physician assistant licensed in this state. 25 * Sec. 8. AS 08.64.275 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 26 (g) A physician assistant applying under (a) of this section shall pay the 27 required fee and shall meet the requirements of AS 08.64.206 and 08.64.279. In 28 addition, the physician assistant shall submit evidence of holding a license to practice 29 in a state or territory of the United States or in a province or territory of Canada. 30 * Sec. 9. AS 08.64.312(c) is amended to read: 31 (c) The board or its designee may exempt a physician, osteopath, [OR] 01 podiatrist, or physician assistant from the requirements of (b) of this section upon an 02 application by the physician, osteopath, [OR] podiatrist, or physician assistant giving 03 evidence satisfactory to the board or its designee that the physician, osteopath, [OR] 04 podiatrist, or physician assistant is unable to comply with the requirements because 05 of extenuating circumstances. However, a person may not be exempted from more 06 than 15 hours of continuing education in a five-year period; a person may not be 07 exempted from the requirement to receive at least two hours of education in pain 08 management and opioid use and addiction unless the person has demonstrated to the 09 satisfaction of the board that the person does not currently hold a valid federal Drug 10 Enforcement Administration registration number. 11 * Sec. 10. AS 08.64.326(a) is amended to read: 12 (a) The board may impose a sanction if the board finds after a hearing that a 13 licensee 14 (1) secured a license through deceit, fraud, or intentional 15 misrepresentation; 16 (2) engaged in deceit, fraud, or intentional misrepresentation while 17 providing professional services or engaging in professional activities; 18 (3) advertised professional services in a false or misleading manner; 19 (4) has been convicted, including conviction based on a guilty plea or 20 plea of nolo contendere, of 21 (A) a class A or unclassified felony or a crime in another 22 jurisdiction with elements similar to a class A or unclassified felony in this 23 jurisdiction; 24 (B) a class B or class C felony or a crime in another jurisdiction 25 with elements similar to a class B or class C felony in this jurisdiction if the 26 felony or other crime is substantially related to the qualifications, functions, or 27 duties of the licensee; or 28 (C) a crime involving the unlawful procurement, sale, 29 prescription, or dispensing of drugs; 30 (5) has procured, sold, prescribed, or dispensed drugs in violation of a 31 law regardless of whether there has been a criminal action or harm to the patient; 01 (6) intentionally or negligently permitted the performance of patient 02 care by persons under the licensee's supervision that does not conform to minimum 03 professional standards even if the patient was not injured; 04 (7) failed to comply with this chapter, a regulation adopted under this 05 chapter, or an order of the board; 06 (8) has demonstrated 07 (A) professional incompetence, gross negligence, or repeated 08 negligent conduct; the board may not base a finding of professional 09 incompetence solely on the basis that a licensee's practice is unconventional or 10 experimental in the absence of demonstrable physical harm to a patient; 11 (B) addiction to, severe dependency on, or habitual overuse of 12 alcohol or other drugs that impairs the licensee's ability to practice safely; 13 (C) unfitness because of physical or mental disability; 14 (9) engaged in unprofessional conduct, in sexual misconduct, or in 15 lewd or immoral conduct in connection with the delivery of professional services to 16 patients; in this paragraph, "sexual misconduct" includes sexual contact, as defined by 17 the board in regulations adopted under this chapter, or attempted sexual contact with a 18 patient outside the scope of generally accepted methods of examination or treatment of 19 the patient, regardless of the patient's consent or lack of consent, during the term of the 20 physician-patient relationship, as defined by the board in regulations adopted under 21 this chapter, unless the patient was the licensee's spouse at the time of the contact or, 22 immediately preceding the physician-patient relationship, was in a dating, courtship, 23 or engagement relationship with the licensee; 24 (10) has violated AS 18.16.010; 25 (11) has violated any code of ethics adopted by regulation by the 26 board; 27 (12) has denied care or treatment to a patient or person seeking 28 assistance from the licensee [PHYSICIAN] if the only reason for the denial is the 29 failure or refusal of the patient to agree to arbitrate as provided in AS 09.55.535(a); 30 (13) has had a license or certificate to practice medicine in another 31 state or territory of the United States, or a province or territory of Canada, denied, 01 suspended, revoked, surrendered while under investigation for an alleged violation, 02 restricted, limited, conditioned, or placed on probation unless the denial, suspension, 03 revocation, or other action was caused by the failure of the licensee to pay fees to that 04 state, territory, or province; or 05 (14) prescribed or dispensed an opioid in excess of the maximum 06 dosage authorized under AS 08.64.363. 07 * Sec. 11. AS 08.64.334 is amended to read: 08 Sec. 08.64.334. Voluntary surrender. The board, at its discretion, may accept 09 the voluntary surrender of a license. A license may not be returned unless the board 10 determines, under regulations adopted by it, that the licensee is competent to resume 11 practice. However, a license may not be returned to the licensee if the voluntary 12 surrender resulted in the dropping or suspension of civil or criminal charges against 13 the physician or physician assistant. 14 * Sec. 12. AS 08.64.336(a) is amended to read: 15 (a) A physician or physician assistant who professionally treats a person 16 licensed to practice medicine or osteopathy in this state for alcoholism or drug 17 addiction, or for mental, emotional, or personality disorders, shall report [IT] to the 18 board if there is probable cause that the person may constitute a danger to the health 19 and welfare of that person's patients or the public if that person continues in practice. 20 The report must state the name and address of the person and the condition found. 21 * Sec. 13. AS 08.64.336(e) is amended to read: 22 (e) A physician, physician assistant, hospital, hospital committee, or private 23 professional organization contracted with under AS 08.64.101(a)(5) to identify, 24 confront, evaluate, and treat individuals licensed under this chapter who abuse 25 addictive substances that in good faith submits a report under this section or 26 participates in an investigation or judicial proceeding related to a report submitted 27 under this section is immune from civil liability for the submission or participation. 28 * Sec. 14. AS 08.64.336(f) is amended to read: 29 (f) A physician, physician assistant, or hospital may not refuse to submit a 30 report under this section or withhold from the board or its investigators evidence 31 related to an investigation under this section on the grounds that the report or evidence 01 (1) concerns a matter that was disclosed in the course of a confidential 02 physician-patient or psychotherapist-patient relationship or during a meeting of a 03 hospital medical staff, governing body, or committee that was exempt from the public 04 meeting requirements of AS 44.62.310; or 05 (2) is required to be kept confidential under AS 18.23.030. 06 * Sec. 15. AS 08.64.360 is amended to read: 07 Sec. 08.64.360. Penalty for practicing without a license or in violation of 08 law. Except for [A PHYSICIAN ASSISTANT OR] a person licensed or authorized 09 under another law of the state who engages in practices for which that person is 10 licensed or authorized under that law, a person practicing medicine or osteopathy in 11 the state without a valid license or permit is guilty of a class A misdemeanor. Each day 12 of illegal practice is a separate offense. 13 * Sec. 16. AS 08.64.370 is amended to read: 14 Sec. 08.64.370. Exceptions to application of chapter. This chapter does not 15 apply to 16 (1) officers in the regular medical service of the armed services of the 17 United States or the United States Public Health Service while in the discharge of their 18 official duties; 19 (2) a physician, [OR] osteopath, or physician assistant licensed in 20 another state who is asked by a physician, [OR] osteopath, or physician assistant 21 licensed in this state to help in the diagnosis or treatment of a case, unless the 22 physician, osteopath, or physician assistant is practicing under AS 08.02.130(b); 23 (3) the practice of the religious tenets of a church; 24 (4) a physician or physician assistant in the regular medical service of 25 the United States Public Health Service or the armed services of the United States 26 volunteering services without pay or other remuneration to a hospital, clinic, medical 27 office, or other medical facility in the state; 28 (5) a person who is certified as a direct-entry midwife by the 29 department under AS 08.65 while engaged in the practice of midwifery whether or not 30 the person accepts compensation for those services; 31 (6) a physician or physician assistant licensed in another state who, 01 under a written agreement with an athletic team located in the state in which the 02 physician or physician assistant is licensed, provides medical services to members of 03 the athletic team while the athletic team is traveling to or from or participating in a 04 sporting event in this state. 05 * Sec. 17. AS 08.64.380(6) is amended to read: 06 (6) "practice of medicine" or "practice of osteopathy" means [:] 07 (A) for a fee, donation, or other consideration, to diagnose, 08 treat, operate on, prescribe for, or administer to [,] any human ailment, 09 blemish, deformity, disease, disfigurement, disorder, injury, or other mental or 10 physical condition; or to attempt to perform or represent that a person is 11 authorized to perform any of the acts set out in this subparagraph; 12 (B) to use or publicly display a title in connection with a 13 person's name in such a manner as to show that the person is willing or 14 qualified to diagnose or treat the sick or injured, including "doctor of 15 medicine," "physician," "M.D.," [OR] "doctor of osteopathic medicine," [OR] 16 "D.O.," "physician assistant," or "P.A." or a specialist designation, 17 including "surgeon," "dermatologist," or a similar title, except that "practice 18 of medicine" or "practice of osteopathy" does not include the use of the 19 title "doctor" by a person holding a doctorate degree who is not a 20 physician or osteopath and who clearly communicates that the person is 21 not a physician or osteopath [IN SUCH A MANNER AS TO SHOW THAT 22 THE PERSON IS WILLING OR QUALIFIED TO DIAGNOSE OR TREAT 23 THE SICK OR INJURED]; 24 * Sec. 18.

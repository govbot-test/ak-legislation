the 06 July 4, 1776, adoption in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, is the date that typically signifies the 07 founding of the United States of America; and 08 WHEREAS the 56 patriots who signed the Declaration of Independence are John 09 Adams, Samuel Adams, Josiah Bartlett, Carter Braxton, Charles Carroll of Carrollton, Samuel 10 Chase, Abraham Clark, George Clymer, William Ellery, William Floyd, Benjamin Franklin, 11 Elbridge Gerry, Button Gwinnett, Lyman Hall, John Hancock, Benjamin Harrison, John Hart, 12 Joseph Hewes, Thomas Heyward, Jr., William Hooper, Stephen Hopkins, Francis Hopkinson, 13 Samuel Huntington, Thomas Jefferson, Francis Lightfoot Lee, Richard Henry Lee, Francis 14 Lewis, Philip Livingston, Thomas Lynch, Jr., Thomas McKean, Arthur Middleton, Lewis 15 Morris, Robert Morris, John Morton, Thomas Nelson, Jr., William Paca, Robert Treat Paine, 16 John Penn, George Read, Caesar Rodney, George Ross, Dr. Benjamin Rush, Edward 01 Rutledge, Roger Sherman, James Smith, Richard Stockton, Thomas Stone, George Taylor, 02 Matthew Thornton, George Walton, William Whipple, William Williams, James Wilson, John 03 Witherspoon, Oliver Wolcott, and George Wythe; and 04 WHEREAS the Declaration of Independence outlines the founding principles of the 05 United States of America by declaring "We hold these truths to be self-evident, that all men 06 are created equal, that they are endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable Rights, that 07 among these are Life, Liberty and the pursuit of Happiness"; and 08 WHEREAS the Declaration of Independence proclaimed timeless principles of 09 profound significance to the history of the United States, including that all people are created 10 equal and endowed with certain inalienable rights, including life, liberty, and the pursuit of 11 happiness, and that a government derives its just powers from the consent of the governed; 12 and 13 WHEREAS the United States was founded on the principle of federalism, which 14 emphasizes a decentralized system of government in which powers are divided between 15 national and state governments, and fosters liberty, accountability, and responsiveness to local 16 communities; and 17 WHEREAS the founding of the United States initiated a journey toward forming "a 18 more perfect Union," marked by historic achievements, struggles, and progress in realizing 19 the nation's founding principles for all citizens; and 20 WHEREAS signing the Declaration of Independence was a critical part of the 21 Revolutionary War, which ended when the Treaty of Paris was signed on September 3, 1783, 22 and granted the United States of America independence from Great Britain; and 23 WHEREAS the state has played a significant role in the history of the United States 24 by contributing to the nation's development, culture, economy, and defense through the efforts 25 and sacrifices of the state's citizens; and 26 WHEREAS the semiquincentennial of the United States offers a unique opportunity 27 for the people of the state to reflect on a shared history, honor the diverse contributions of the 28 nation's citizens, and continually strive to create a more perfect union for future generations; 29 and 30 WHEREAS commemorative activities across the state will provide educational 31 opportunities, foster civic engagement, encourage community participation, and strengthen 01 appreciation for the history, federalist principles, and foundational ideals on which both the 02 nation and this state were built; and 03 WHEREAS over the past 250 years there have been many efforts to expand the rights 04 of all citizens to uphold that self-evident truth, including ending slavery, expanding voting 05 rights to women and citizens of all races, expanding civil rights for all citizens, pursuing equal 06 rights by ratifying the Equal Rights Amendment, continuing efforts to respect and 07 acknowledge the Indigenous communities who, for thousands of years, have stewarded the 08 land that comprises the United States, and much more; and 09 WHEREAS July 4, 2026, will be the 250th anniversary of the signing of the 10 Declaration of Independence; 11 BE IT RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature honors the 250th anniversary of 12 the Declaration of Independence and the 250th anniversary of the founding of the United 13 States of America; and be it 14 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature encourages all citizens, 15 communities, educational institutions, civic organizations, and businesses in the state 16 to participate in commemorative events and activities that reflect on the nation's history, 17 celebrate the nation's achievements, and reaffirm a commitment to the ideals of liberty, 18 equality, federalism, and self-governance; and be it 19 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature encourages educational 20 institutions across the state to enhance civics education to ensure that future generations 21 understand the importance of active civic participation and the principles of federalism and 22 self-governance that protect liberty and promote a responsive government.

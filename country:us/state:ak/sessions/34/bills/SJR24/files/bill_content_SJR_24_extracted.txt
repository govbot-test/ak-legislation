and recognizing the importance of cooperation between the United States of America and the Kingdom of Denmark in the Arctic. 00 SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION NO. 24 01 Recognizing and honoring the relationship between the state and Greenland; and 02 recognizing the importance of cooperation between the United States of America and the 03 Kingdom of Denmark in the Arctic. 04 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA: 05 WHEREAS the state and Greenland are Arctic neighbors, each blessed with 06 extraordinary natural environments, valuable natural resources, and Indigenous cultures with 07 histories stretching back millennia; and 08 WHEREAS the Alaska Native peoples and the Inuit peoples of Greenland share 09 cultural, linguistic, and historical connections that continue to shape Arctic identity, 10 resilience, and stewardship; and 11 WHEREAS the state and Greenland face common Arctic-related challenges, 12 including climate change, melting sea ice, coastal erosion, food security, infrastructure needs, 13 and the high cost of living in remote communities; and 14 WHEREAS the United States of America and the Kingdom of Denmark, of which 15 Greenland is an autonomous territory, maintain a long-standing alliance grounded in 01 democratic values, mutual defense, and international cooperation; and 02 WHEREAS the United States of American and the Kingdom of Denmark are 03 founding members of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, and Greenland plays a critical 04 role in the collective security of the North Atlantic and Arctic regions; and 05 WHEREAS the presence of Pituffik Space Base in Greenland underscores 06 Greenland's strategic importance to Arctic and global security and its relevance to the state's 07 role in United States Arctic defense; and 08 WHEREAS the state and Greenland share strategic interests in Arctic maritime 09 safety, search and rescue, fisheries management, environmental protection, scientific research, 10 and responsible resource development; and 11 WHEREAS cooperation among Arctic peoples and governments strengthens 12 economic opportunity, advances innovation, and supports sustainable development 13 throughout the circumpolar North; and 14 WHEREAS the state and Greenland benefit from collaboration through international 15 Arctic institutions and forums, including the Arctic Council, which promotes peaceful 16 cooperation, scientific research, and the well-being of Arctic residents; and 17 WHEREAS growing global attention to the Arctic heightens the importance of strong 18 relationships among Arctic regions, including the state and Greenland, to ensure that 19 development proceeds in a manner that respects local communities and fragile ecosystems; 20 BE IT RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature recognizes and honors the 21 relationship between the state and Greenland as an essential Arctic partnership grounded in 22 shared geography, culture, and strategic interests; and be it 23 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature affirms the importance of 24 continued cooperation between the United States of America and the Kingdom of Denmark, 25 including Greenland, on matters of Arctic security, economic development, environmental 26 protection, and scientific research; and be it 27 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature encourages strengthened 28 collaboration between the state and Greenland in areas including Indigenous exchange, 29 education, renewable energy, fisheries, transportation, and emergency response; and be it 30 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature expresses its commitment 31 to fostering friendship, mutual respect, and long-term cooperation between the peoples of the 01 state and Greenland in support of a peaceful, prosperous, and resilient Arctic. 02 COPIES of this resolution shall be sent to the Honorable Donald J. Trump, President 03 of the United States; the Honourable Mette Frederiksen, Prime Minister of the Kingdom of 04 Denmark; the Honourable Jens-Frederik Nielsen, Prime Minister of Greenland; the Honorable 05 Marco Rubio, United States Secretary of State; the Honorable Pete Hegseth, United States 06 Secretary of Defense; General Gregory M. Guillot, Commander, United States Northern 07 Command; and the Honorable Lisa Murkowski and the Honorable Dan Sullivan, U.S. 08 Senators, and the Honorable Nicholas Begich, U.S. Representative, members of the Alaska 09 delegation in Congress.

and celebrating the enduring contributions of the state's regional educational attendance areas to public education, local leadership, and community life in rural areas of the state. 00Enrolled SCR 17 01 Recognizing the 50th anniversary of the state's regional educational attendance areas; and 02 celebrating the enduring contributions of the state's regional educational attendance areas to 03 public education, local leadership, and community life in rural areas of the state. 04 _______________ 05 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA: 06 WHEREAS Article VII, sec. 1, Constitution of the State of Alaska, requires the 07 legislature to establish and maintain a system of public schools open to all children of the 08 state; and 09 WHEREAS, in 1975, the Alaska State Legislature enacted landmark reforms that 10 established the regional educational attendance areas in rural areas of the state, creating a new 11 framework for public education in the unorganized borough; and 12 WHEREAS the landmark "Molly Hootch" litigation, Tobeluk v. Lind, 589 P.2d 873 13 (Alaska 1979), culminated in a 1976 consent decree requiring the state to provide local 14 secondary schools in rural communities, reinforcing the state's constitutional obligation to 15 offer equitable educational opportunity and shaping the modern development of locally 16 governed public education in rural areas of the state, including the regional educational 17 attendance areas; and 01 WHEREAS the regional educational attendance area structure was designed to 02 replace centralized administration with a model rooted in community voice, regional decision- 03 making, and public accountability; and 04 WHEREAS state law delegates authority to regional school boards in each regional 05 educational attendance area to operate public schools in those areas in accordance with state 06 law, recognizing regional educational attendance areas as full participants in the state's public 07 education system; and 08 WHEREAS state law provides that regional educational attendance areas should, as 09 far as practicable, reflect integrated socioeconomic, linguistic, and cultural communities, 10 while also considering transportation and communication networks, demonstrating an early 11 and important legislative recognition that effective education governance in rural areas of the 12 state must reflect place, people, and practical realities; and 13 WHEREAS regional educational attendance areas are operated on an areawide basis 14 under the management and control of elected regional school boards, creating a durable 15 system of democratic local governance in communities across the unorganized borough; and 16 WHEREAS state law requires regional school boards to establish community 17 advisory school boards in qualifying communities, strengthening community participation and 18 helping ensure that school leadership remains connected to local priorities and students' lived 19 realities; and 20 WHEREAS, for five decades, regional educational attendance area school districts 21 have educated generations of state residents, served tens of thousands of students, sustained 22 schools in remote and roadless communities, and helped preserve the principle that a child's 23 access to public education should not depend on whether that child lives in an urban center or 24 a rural village; and 25 WHEREAS regional educational attendance areas have contributed to the state's civic 26 life by fostering local leadership, including school board members, educators, support staff, 27 and community advocates whose service has strengthened both schools and communities; and 28 WHEREAS regional educational attendance area educators, staff, families, tribal 29 leaders, elders, and community members have worked, often under difficult conditions and 30 across immense distances, to support students and keep schools operating through changing 31 economic, technological, and social conditions; and 01 WHEREAS the 50th anniversary of the establishment of regional educational 02 attendance areas is an appropriate occasion for the legislature to recognize the vision behind 03 the creation of regional educational attendance areas and to honor the people who have 04 sustained and improved the regional educational attendance areas over time; 05 BE IT RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature honors the 50th anniversary of 06 the regional educational attendance areas and recognizes the regional educational attendance 07 areas as a foundational part of the state's public education system and a lasting expression of 08 the state's commitment to educational access, local governance, and rural communities; and 09 be it 10 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature expresses gratitude to the 11 students, families, elders, tribes, regional school board members, advisory school board 12 members, teachers, administrators, and school staff whose work and leadership have shaped 13 the regional educational attendance areas across five decades; and be it 14 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature reaffirms its commitment 15 to strong public schools in every region of the state and to continued partnership with regional 16 educational attendance area districts, tribes, and communities in support of student success. 17 COPIES of this resolution shall be sent to the Honorable Dr. Deena Bishop, 18 Commissioner, Department of Education and Early Development; the State Board of 19 Education and Early Development; the Association of Alaska School Boards; each Regional 20 Educational Attendance Area school district in the state; the Honorable Lisa Murkowski and 21 the Honorable Dan Sullivan, U.S. Senators, and the Honorable Nicholas Begich, U.S. 22 Representative, members of the Alaska delegation in Congress.

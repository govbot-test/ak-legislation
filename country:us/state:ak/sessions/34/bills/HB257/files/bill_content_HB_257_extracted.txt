AS 42.05 is amended by adding a new section to read: 05 Sec. 42.05.323. Portable solar generation devices. (a) A portable solar 06 generation device is exempt from utility interconnection and net metering program 07 requirements adopted by the commission if the device 08 (1) has a maximum power output of not more than 1,200 watts; 09 (2) meets the standards of the most recent version of the National 10 Electrical Code; and 11 (3) is certified by Underwriters Laboratories or an equivalent 12 nationally recognized testing laboratory. 13 (b) An electric utility may not require a customer using a portable solar 14 generation device that meets the requirements of (a) of this section to 01 (1) obtain the electric utility's approval before installing or using the 02 device; 03 (2) pay a fee or charge related to the device; or 04 (3) install additional controls or equipment beyond what is integrated 05 into the device. 06 (c) An electric utility is not liable for any damage or injury caused by a 07 portable solar generation device that meets the requirements in (a) of this section. 08 (d) In this section, "portable solar generation device" means a moveable 09 photovoltaic generation device that is designed to be 10 (1) connected to a building's electrical system through a standard 120- 11 volt alternating current outlet; and 12 (2) used primarily to offset part of an electric utility customer's 13 electricity consumption.

relating to data sharing by the 03 Department of Administration; relating to electronic driver's licenses and permits; 04 relating to federal benefits for children in need of aid; and providing for an effective 05 date." 06 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA: 07 * Section 1. AS 18.65.310(a) is amended to read: 08 (a) Upon payment of a $15 fee, the department shall issue a physical card 09 identical to the motor vehicle operator's license provided for in AS 28.15.111, except 10 that the card shall be of a different color and shall state in bold type letters across the 11 face of the card [IT] that the card [IT] is for identification purposes only. Upon 12 request of a person and upon payment of an additional $20 fee, the department may 13 issue an identification card under this section that is federally compliant. 01 * Sec. 2. AS 18.65.310 is amended by adding new subsections to read: 02 (s) Upon application, the department may issue an electronic identification 03 card to a person who possesses a physical identification card. A person issued an 04 electronic identification card shall retain the physical identification card issued by the 05 department. 06 (t) The department shall adopt regulations for the issuance and use of 07 electronic identification cards. 08 (u) Presentation of an electronic identification card on a mobile electronic 09 device for identification purposes does not constitute consent for a peace officer or 10 other authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety to search, view, or 11 access other contents of the mobile electronic device. A peace officer or other 12 authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety presented with an 13 electronic identification card shall promptly return the mobile electronic device to the 14 person once the peace officer or representative verifies the identity of the person. 15 (v) A peace officer or other authorized representative of the Department of 16 Public Safety presented with an electronic identification card on a mobile electronic 17 device under this section is immune from liability resulting from damage to the 18 device, except that a peace officer or other authorized representative of the 19 Department of Public Safety may be liable for civil damages that are the result of the 20 peace officer's or representative's intentional misconduct. 21 * Sec. 3. AS 18.65.320(b) is amended to read: 22 (b) A person whose identification card has been cancelled shall return the 23 physical card to the department. Failure to return a cancelled card within 10 days after 24 receiving notice of the cancellation is a violation punishable by a fine of up to $100. 25 * Sec. 4. AS 28.05.021 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 26 (c) The commissioner of administration may enter into an agreement with a 27 law enforcement agency, governmental agency or program, or data exchange service, 28 including the National Driver Register, the Problem Driver Pointer System, the 29 Commercial Driver's License Information System, the State-to-State Verification 30 Service, and the Driver's License Data Verification Service. 31 * Sec. 5. AS 28.05.051 is amended to read: 01 Sec. 28.05.051. Suspended or revoked documents. (a) When the department 02 suspends or revokes a vehicle registration, certificate of registration, registration plate, 03 permit, or certificate of title or suspends, cancels, or revokes a license, the owner or 04 person in possession of the document shall, immediately upon receiving notice of the 05 suspension, revocation, or cancellation, mail or deliver the registration, certificate, 06 plate, permit, or physical license to the department. 07 (b) The commissioner, officers and employees of the department designated 08 by the commissioner, judges and employees of a court, and all peace officers [,] may 09 take possession of a certificate of title, registration, or physical license issued by this 10 jurisdiction that has been revoked, canceled, limited, or suspended, or is fictitious, 11 stolen, or altered. 12 * Sec. 6. AS 28.05.068(a) is amended to read: 13 (a) If the department conveys, distributes, or communicates data to be used in 14 a database, index, pointer system, or any other system managed by an entity other than 15 the department [, INCLUDING THE AMERICAN ASSOCIATION OF MOTOR 16 VEHICLE ADMINISTRATORS,] to comply with the requirements of P.L. 109-13, 17 Division B (REAL ID Act of 2005), the department may not convey, distribute, or 18 communicate to that entity any data not required to be conveyed, distributed, or 19 communicated for the state to be certified by the United States Department of 20 Homeland Security to be in compliance with the requirements of P.L. 109-13, 21 Division B (REAL ID Act of 2005). 22 * Sec. 7. AS 28.05.068(b) is amended to read: 23 (b) Notwithstanding (a) of this section, the department may [NOT] convey, 24 distribute, or communicate data to a private entity only to confirm the information 25 of an individual who has voluntarily provided the individual's state driver's 26 license or identification card [ANY SCANNED OR STORED DOCUMENTS 27 COLLECTED IN ORDER] to carry out the provisions of AS 28.15.151(g) 28 [AS 28.15]. 29 * Sec. 8. AS 28.15.011(b) is amended to read: 30 (b) Every person exercising the person's privilege to drive, or exercising any 31 degree of physical control of a motor vehicle on [UPON] a highway, vehicular way or 01 area, or other public property in this state, is required to have in the possession of the 02 person a valid physical or electronic Alaska driver's license issued under the 03 provisions of this chapter for the type or class of vehicle driven, unless expressly 04 exempted by law from this requirement. 05 * Sec. 9. AS 28.15 is amended by adding a new section to read: 06 Sec. 28.15.126. Electronic licenses and permits. (a) Upon application, the 07 department may issue an electronic driver's license or permit under this chapter to a 08 person who possesses a physical driver's license or permit. A person issued an 09 electronic driver's license or permit shall retain the physical driver's license or permit 10 issued by the department. 11 (b) The commissioner shall adopt regulations for the issuance and use of 12 electronic driver's licenses and permits issued under this chapter. The commissioner 13 may adopt regulations to require a driver to possess a physical driver's license on the 14 driver's person in certain circumstances. 15 (c) If a person's physical driver's license is seized, revoked, canceled, limited, 16 restricted, or suspended under this chapter, the department shall display a notice 17 regarding the seizure, revocation, cancellation, limitation, restriction, or suspension 18 across the front of the person's electronic driver's license. 19 * Sec. 10. AS 28.15.131(a) is amended to read: 20 (a) A licensee shall have the licensee's physical or electronic driver's license 21 in immediate possession at all times when driving a motor vehicle, and shall present 22 the license for inspection upon the demand of a peace officer or other authorized 23 representative of the Department of Public Safety identified as such to the licensee by 24 the officer or representative. However, a person charged with violating this section 25 may not be convicted if the person produces in court or in the office of the arresting or 26 citing officer, a driver's license previously issued to the person that was valid at the 27 time of the person's arrest or citation. 28 * Sec. 11. AS 28.15.131 is amended by adding new subsections to read: 29 (c) Presentation of an electronic driver's license or permit on a mobile 30 electronic device under this chapter does not constitute consent for a peace officer or 31 other authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety to search, view, or 01 access other contents of the mobile electronic device. A peace officer or other 02 authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety presented with an 03 electronic driver's license or permit shall promptly return the mobile electronic device 04 to the person once the peace officer or representative verifies the identity and license 05 or permit status of the person. 06 (d) A peace officer or other authorized representative of the Department of 07 Public Safety presented with an electronic driver's license or permit on a mobile 08 electronic device under this chapter is immune from liability resulting from damage to 09 the device, except that a peace officer or other authorized representative of the 10 Department of Public Safety may be liable for civil damages that are the result of the 11 peace officer's or representative's intentional misconduct. 12 * Sec. 12. AS 28.15.151(a) is amended to read: 13 (a) The department shall maintain a file of 14 (1) every driver's license application, license or permit, and duplicate 15 driver's license issued by the department [IT]; 16 (2) every license that has been suspended, revoked, canceled, limited, 17 restricted, or denied, and the reasons for those actions; 18 (3) all accident reports required to be forwarded to the department 19 under this title; [AND] 20 (4) every disqualification of an individual from operating a 21 commercial motor vehicle; and 22 (5) every application for an electronic driver's license or permit 23 and the electronic driver's licenses and permits issued by the department. 24 * Sec. 13. AS 28.15.151 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 25 (g) The department may, upon the request of a person or the person's designee, 26 furnish information about the person maintained by the department under this section 27 to 28 (1) a nonprofit organization or governmental or tribal entity approved 29 by the United States Social Security Administration to facilitate driver's license data 30 verification. An entity that receives information under this subsection shall use the 31 information only for driver's license data verification purposes and may not disclose, 01 retain, sell, or use the information for any other purpose. The entity shall maintain 02 reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the 03 confidentiality, integrity, and security of the information; in this paragraph, "nonprofit 04 organization" means an organization that qualifies for exemption from taxation under 05 26 U.S.C. 501(c)(3) or (4) (Internal Revenue Code); 06 (2) an entity participating in the Transportation Security 07 Administration's Registered Traveler Programs; information provided under this 08 paragraph shall be used for lawful purposes and is not limited to use within a program 09 if the entity receives the information to facilitate driver's license or identification card 10 data verification and the individual has voluntarily provided the individual's driver's 11 license or identification card. 12 * Sec. 14. AS 28.15.165(b) is amended to read: 13 (b) After reading the notice under (a) of this section, the law enforcement 14 officer shall seize the person's physical driver's license if the physical driver's 15 license [IT] is in the person's possession and shall deliver the physical driver's 16 license [IT] to the department with a sworn report describing the circumstances under 17 which the physical driver's license [IT] was seized. If the person was operating a 18 commercial motor vehicle, the officer shall order the person out of service under 19 AS 28.33.130. 20 * Sec. 15. AS 28.15.166(a) is amended to read: 21 (a) A person who has received a notice under AS 28.15.165(a) may make a 22 written request for administrative review of the department's action under 23 AS 28.15.165(c) or for limited license privileges under AS 28.15.165(d). If the 24 person's physical driver's license has not been previously surrendered to the 25 department, the physical driver's license [IT] shall be surrendered to the department 26 at the time the request for review is made. 27 * Sec. 16. AS 28.15.182(d) is amended to read: 28 (d) When imposing a limitation under (c) of this section, the court shall 29 (1) require the surrender of the physical driver's license; and 30 (2) issue to the person a certificate valid for the duration of the 31 limitation specifying the terms of the limited license. 01 * Sec. 17. AS 28.15.183(b) is amended to read: 02 (b) After reading the notice under (a) of this section, the peace officer shall 03 seize the person's physical driver's license or permit if the physical driver's license 04 or permit [IT] is in the person's possession and shall deliver the physical driver's 05 license or permit [IT] to the department with a sworn report describing the 06 circumstances under which the physical driver's license or permit [IT] was seized. 07 * Sec. 18. AS 28.15.184(a) is amended to read: 08 (a) A person who has received a notice under AS 28.15.183(a) may make a 09 written request for administrative review of the department's action. If the person's 10 physical driver's license or permit has not been previously surrendered to the 11 department, the physical driver's license or permit [IT] shall be surrendered to the 12 department at the time the request for review is made. 13 * Sec. 19. AS 28.15.187(b) is amended to read: 14 (b) After reading the notice under (a) of this section, the peace officer shall 15 seize the person's physical driver's license if the physical driver's license [IT] is in 16 the person's possession and shall deliver the physical driver's license [IT] to the 17 department with a sworn report describing the circumstances under which the 18 physical driver's license [IT] was seized. 19 * Sec. 20. AS 28.15.189(a) is amended to read: 20 (a) A person who has received a notice under AS 28.15.187(a) may make a 21 written request for administrative review of the department's action. If the person's 22 physical driver's license has not been previously surrendered to the department, the 23 physical driver's license [IT] shall be surrendered to the department at the time the 24 request for review is made. 25 * Sec. 21. AS 28.15.191(c) is amended to read: 26 (c) A court that suspends, revokes, or limits a driver's license shall require the 27 surrender of the physical license, and shall immediately forward the physical driver's 28 license [IT] to the department with the record of conviction and notification of the 29 effective date of the suspension, revocation, or limitation as determined under 30 AS 28.15.211(b). 31 * Sec. 22. AS 28.15.191(g) is amended to read: 01 (g) A court that has ordered a person to refrain from consuming alcoholic 02 beverages as part of a sentence for conviction of a crime under AS 28.35.030, 03 28.35.032, or a similar municipal ordinance or as a condition of probation or parole 04 following a conviction under those sections or a similar municipal ordinance, or as a 05 condition of probation or parole for any other crime shall 06 (1) require the surrender of the person's physical license and 07 identification card and forward the physical license and identification card to the 08 department; 09 (2) report the order to the department within two days; and 10 (3) inform the person that the person's license and identification card 11 are subject to cancellation under AS 28.15.161 and AS 18.65.310 and, if the person is 12 otherwise qualified to receive a license or identification card, when the person obtains 13 a new license or identification card, the license or identification card must list the 14 restriction imposed by AS 04.16.160 for the period of probation or parole. 15 * Sec. 23. AS 28.15.191(h) is amended to read: 16 (h) The board of parole shall notify the department within two days whenever 17 a person has been ordered to refrain from consuming alcoholic beverages as a 18 condition of parole, shall require the person to surrender the person's physical license 19 and identification card, and shall inform the person that the person's license and 20 identification card are subject to cancellation under AS 28.15.161 and AS 18.65.310, 21 and that, if the person is otherwise qualified to receive a license or identification card, 22 when the person obtains a new license or identification card, the license or 23 identification card must list the restriction imposed by AS 04.16.160. 24 * Sec. 24. AS 28.15.201(b) is amended to read: 25 (b) A court imposing a limitation under (a) of this section shall 26 (1) require certification of employment; 27 (2) require proof of enrollment in and compliance with or completion 28 of an alcoholism treatment program when appropriate; 29 (3) require the surrender of the physical driver's license; and 30 (4) issue to the licensee a certificate valid for the duration of the 31 limitation. 01 * Sec. 25. AS 28.20.560(a) is amended to read: 02 (a) A person whose license or registration is suspended under any provision of 03 this chapter, or whose policy of insurance or bond, when required under this chapter, 04 is canceled or terminated, shall immediately return the person's physical license or 05 registration to the department. If a person fails to return the license or registration to 06 the department, the department, through the commissioner of public safety, shall 07 immediately direct a peace officer to obtain possession of the license or registration 08 [IT] and to return the license or registration [IT] to the department. 09 * Sec. 26. AS 28.22.041(d) is amended to read: 10 (d) When imposing a limitation under this section, the department shall 11 (1) require the surrender of the driver's physical license; and 12 (2) issue to the licensee a certificate valid for the duration of the 13 limitation. 14 * Sec. 27. AS 28.33.100 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 15 (j) Upon application, the department may issue an electronic commercial 16 driver's license under AS 28.15.126. 17 * Sec. 28. AS 28.33.140(i) is amended to read: 18 (i) In addition to the requirements of AS 28.15.191, a court that disqualifies a 19 person from driving a commercial motor vehicle shall require the surrender of the 20 physical license, and shall immediately forward the physical license to the department 21 with the record of conviction and notification of the effective date of the 22 disqualification. If the disqualification occurs by administrative action as described in 23 (a) of this section, the person disqualified from driving shall surrender the physical 24 license to the department. 25 * Sec. 29. AS 28.35.034 is amended to read: 26 Sec. 28.35.034. Surrender of license or permit. A person whose license or 27 permit to operate or drive a motor vehicle has been revoked under AS 28.15.165 or 28 28.15.181 shall surrender the physical license or permit to the department on receipt 29 of notice of the revocation. After the period of revocation has expired, the person may 30 make application for a new license as provided by law. 31 * Sec. 30. AS 33.16.150(i) is amended to read: 01 (i) In addition to other conditions of parole imposed under this section, for a 02 prisoner who is serving a sentence for an offense involving the use of alcohol and 03 whom the board has ordered to refrain from possessing or consuming alcoholic 04 beverages, the board shall require the surrender of the person's physical driver's 05 license or identification card, forward the physical license or identification card to the 06 department, and impose as a condition of parole that, if the parolee is eligible for a 07 driver's license or identification card, the parolee shall apply to the department for a 08 new license or identification card with a restriction imposed on the person under 09 AS 04.16.160. The board shall notify the department of the board's order under this 10 subsection by providing a copy of the board's order. Upon discharge from parole, the 11 board shall notify the department of the parolee's discharge. In this subsection, 12 "department" means the Department of Administration. 13 * Sec. 31. AS 47.10 is amended by adding a new section to read: 14 Sec. 44.10.117. Federal benefits. (a) The department may not apply for, 15 receive, use, or expend a federal benefit for the purpose of reimbursing the state for 16 the cost of foster care, placement, or other services provided to a child. 17 (b) The department shall determine whether a child in the custody of the state 18 is receiving or may be eligible to receive federal benefits. If the department determines 19 that a child may be eligible for federal benefits, the department shall assist the child or 20 the child's legal representative in applying for the federal benefits. 21 (c) Any federal benefits received on behalf of a child in state custody must be 22 held in trust for the exclusive benefit of the child in an account established for the 23 child. Federal benefits held in trust may only be used for expenditures that directly 24 benefit the child and that are beyond the benefits for which the department is obligated 25 or required to pay. Money held in trust under this subsection shall be invested by the 26 commissioner of revenue in accordance with AS 37.10.070. 27 (d) Upon termination of the department's responsibility for a child, the 28 department shall release any money remaining to the child's credit according to the 29 requirements of the funding source or, in the absence of any requirements, shall 30 release the remaining money to the child once the child has reached 18 years of age or 31 is emancipated. 01 (e) The department shall provide to the child, if age appropriate, the child's 02 guardian ad litem, the child's attorney, and the court an annual accounting of funds 03 held in trust under this section. 04 (f) The department shall adopt regulations necessary to implement 05 AS 47.10.980, including procedures for safeguarding funds, notifying eligible 06 children, and ensuring fiduciary compliance. 07 (g) In this section, "federal benefit" includes a social security survivor or 08 dependent benefit, supplemental security income benefit, veteran benefit payable to or 09 on behalf of a child, and any other federal monetary benefit intended for the direct 10 support of a child. 11 * Sec. 32. This Act takes effect immediately under AS 01.10.070(c).

Enrolled SB 237: Relating to the powers of the commissioner of administration with respect to motor vehicles; relating to electronic identification cards; relating to data sharing by the Department of Administration; relating to electronic driver's licenses and permits; and providing for an effective date. 

00Enrolled SB 237                                                                                                         
01 Relating to the powers of the commissioner of administration with respect to motor vehicles;                            
02 relating to electronic identification cards; relating to data sharing by the Department of                              
03 Administration; relating to electronic driver's licenses and permits; and providing for an                              
04 effective date.                                                                                                         
05                           _______________                                                                               
06    * Section 1. AS 18.65.310(a) is amended to read:                                                                     
07 (a)  Upon payment of a $15 fee, the department shall issue a physical card                                            
08 identical to the motor vehicle operator's license provided for in AS 28.15.111, except                                  
09 that the card shall be of a different color and shall state in bold type letters across the                             
10       face of the card [IT] that the card [IT] is for identification purposes only. Upon                            
11       request of a person and upon payment of an additional $20 fee, the department may                                 
12       issue an identification card under this section that is federally compliant.                                      
13    * Sec. 2. AS 18.65.310 is amended by adding new subsections to read:                                                 
14            (s)  Upon application, the department may issue an electronic identification                                 



01 card to a person who possesses a physical identification card. A person issued an                                       
02 electronic identification card shall retain the physical identification card issued by the                              
03       department.                                                                                                       
04 (t)  The department shall adopt regulations for the issuance and use of                                                 
05       electronic identification cards.                                                                                  
06 (u)  Presentation of an electronic identification card on a mobile electronic                                           
07 device for identification purposes does not constitute consent for a peace officer or                                   
08 other authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety to search, view, or                                  
09 access other contents of the mobile electronic device. A peace officer or other                                         
10 authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety presented with an                                          
11 electronic identification card shall promptly return the mobile electronic device to the                                
12       person once the peace officer or representative verifies the identity of the person.                              
13 (v)  A peace officer or other authorized representative of the Department of                                            
14 Public Safety presented with an electronic identification card on a mobile electronic                                   
15 device under this section is immune from liability resulting from damage to the                                         
16 device, except that a peace officer or other authorized representative of the                                           
17 Department of Public Safety may be liable for civil damages that are the result of the                                  
18       peace officer's or representative's intentional misconduct.                                                       
19    * Sec. 3. AS 18.65.320(b) is amended to read:                                                                        
20 (b)  A person whose identification card has been cancelled shall return the                                             
21 physical card to the department. Failure to return a cancelled card within 10 days after                              
22       receiving notice of the cancellation is a violation punishable by a fine of up to $100.                           
23    * Sec. 4. AS 28.05.021 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:                                                
24 (c)  The commissioner of administration may enter into an agreement with a                                              
25 law enforcement agency, governmental agency or program, or data exchange service,                                       
26 including the National Driver Register, the Problem Driver Pointer System, the                                          
27 Commercial Driver's License Information System, the State-to-State Verification                                         
28       Service, and the Driver's License Data Verification Service.                                                      
29    * Sec. 5. AS 28.05.051 is amended to read:                                                                           
30 Sec. 28.05.051. Suspended or revoked documents. (a) When the department                                                 
31 suspends or revokes a vehicle registration, certificate of registration, registration plate,                            



01 permit, or certificate of title or suspends, cancels, or revokes a license, the owner or                                
02 person in possession of the document shall, immediately upon receiving notice of the                                    
03 suspension, revocation, or cancellation, mail or deliver the registration, certificate,                                 
04       plate, permit, or physical license to the department.                                                           
05 (b)  The commissioner, officers and employees of the department designated                                              
06 by the commissioner, judges and employees of a court, and all peace officers [,] may                                    
07 take possession of a certificate of title, registration, or physical license issued by this                           
08 jurisdiction that has been revoked, canceled, limited, or suspended, or is fictitious,                                  
09       stolen, or altered.                                                                                               
10    * Sec. 6. AS 28.05.068(a) is amended to read:                                                                        
11 (a)  If the department conveys, distributes, or communicates data to be used in                                         
12 a database, index, pointer system, or any other system managed by an entity other than                                  
13 the department [, INCLUDING THE AMERICAN ASSOCIATION OF MOTOR                                                           
14 VEHICLE ADMINISTRATORS,] to comply with the requirements of P.L. 109-13,                                                
15 Division B (REAL ID Act of 2005), the department may not convey, distribute, or                                         
16 communicate to that entity any data not required to be conveyed, distributed, or                                        
17 communicated for the state to be certified by the United States Department of                                           
18 Homeland Security to be in compliance with the requirements of P.L. 109-13,                                             
19       Division B (REAL ID Act of 2005).                                                                                 
20    * Sec. 7. AS 28.05.068(b) is amended to read:                                                                        
21 (b)  Notwithstanding (a) of this section, the department may [NOT] convey,                                              
22 distribute, or communicate data to a private entity only to confirm the information                                 
23 of an individual who has voluntarily provided the individual's state driver's                                         
24 license or identification card [ANY SCANNED OR STORED DOCUMENTS                                                       
25 COLLECTED IN ORDER] to carry out the provisions of AS 28.15.151(g)                                                    
26       [AS 28.15].                                                                                                       
27    * Sec. 8. AS 28.15.011(b) is amended to read:                                                                        
28 (b)  Every person exercising the person's privilege to drive, or exercising any                                         
29 degree of physical control of a motor vehicle on [UPON] a highway, vehicular way or                                   
30 area, or other public property in this state, is required to have in the possession of the                              
31 person a valid physical or electronic Alaska driver's license issued under the                                        



01       provisions of this chapter for the type or class of vehicle driven, unless expressly                              
02       exempted by law from this requirement.                                                                            
03    * Sec. 9. AS 28.15 is amended by adding a new section to read:                                                       
04 Sec. 28.15.126. Electronic licenses and permits. (a) Upon application, the                                              
05 department may issue an electronic driver's license or permit under this chapter to a                                   
06 person who possesses a physical driver's license or permit. A person issued an                                          
07 electronic driver's license or permit shall retain the physical driver's license or permit                              
08       issued by the department.                                                                                         
09            (b)  The commissioner shall adopt regulations for the issuance and use of                                    
10 electronic driver's licenses and permits issued under this chapter. The commissioner                                    
11 may adopt regulations to require a driver to possess a physical driver's license on the                                 
12       driver's person in certain circumstances.                                                                         
13 (c)  If a person's physical driver's license is seized, revoked, canceled, limited,                                     
14 restricted, or suspended under this chapter, the department shall display a notice                                      
15 regarding the seizure, revocation, cancellation, limitation, restriction, or suspension                                 
16       across the front of the person's electronic driver's license.                                                     
17    * Sec. 10. AS 28.15.131(a) is amended to read:                                                                       
18 (a)  A licensee shall have the licensee's physical or electronic driver's license                                     
19 in immediate possession at all times when driving a motor vehicle, and shall present                                    
20 the license for inspection upon the demand of a peace officer or other authorized                                       
21 representative of the Department of Public Safety identified as such to the licensee by                                 
22 the officer or representative. However, a person charged with violating this section                                    
23 may not be convicted if the person produces in court or in the office of the arresting or                               
24 citing officer, a driver's license previously issued to the person that was valid at the                                
25       time of the person's arrest or citation.                                                                          
26    * Sec. 11. AS 28.15.131 is amended by adding new subsections to read:                                                
27 (c)  Presentation of an electronic driver's license or permit on a mobile                                               
28 electronic device under this chapter does not constitute consent for a peace officer or                                 
29 other authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety to search, view, or                                  
30 access other contents of the mobile electronic device. A peace officer or other                                         
31 authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety presented with an                                          



01 electronic driver's license or permit shall promptly return the mobile electronic device                                
02 to the person once the peace officer or representative verifies the identity and license                                
03       or permit status of the person.                                                                                   
04 (d)  A peace officer or other authorized representative of the Department of                                            
05 Public Safety presented with an electronic driver's license or permit on a mobile                                       
06 electronic device under this chapter is immune from liability resulting from damage to                                  
07 the device, except that a peace officer or other authorized representative of the                                       
08 Department of Public Safety may be liable for civil damages that are the result of the                                  
09       peace officer's or representative's intentional misconduct.                                                       
10    * Sec. 12. AS 28.15.151(a) is amended to read:                                                                       
11            (a)  The department shall maintain a file of                                                                 
12 (1)  every driver's license application, license or permit, and duplicate                                               
13       driver's license issued by the department [IT];                                                                 
14 (2)  every license that has been suspended, revoked, canceled, limited,                                                 
15       restricted, or denied, and the reasons for those actions;                                                         
16 (3)  all accident reports required to be forwarded to the department                                                    
17       under this title; [AND]                                                                                           
18 (4)  every disqualification of an individual from operating a                                                           
19       commercial motor vehicle; and                                                                                   
20 (5)  every application for an electronic driver's license or permit                                                   
21       and the electronic driver's licenses and permits issued by the department.                                      
22    * Sec. 13. AS 28.15.151 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:                                               
23 (g)  The department may, upon the request of a person or the person's designee,                                         
24 furnish information about the person maintained by the department under this section                                    
25       to                                                                                                                
26 (1)  a nonprofit organization or governmental or tribal entity approved                                                 
27 by the United States Social Security Administration to facilitate driver's license data                                 
28 verification; an entity that receives information under this paragraph shall use the                                    
29 information only for driver's license data verification purposes and may not disclose,                                  
30 retain, sell, or use the information for any other purpose; the entity shall maintain                                   
31 reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the                                            



01 confidentiality, integrity, and security of the information; in this paragraph, "nonprofit                              
02 organization" means an organization that qualifies for exemption from taxation under                                    
03       26 U.S.C. 501(c)(3) or (4) (Internal Revenue Code);                                                               
04 (2)  an entity participating in the Transportation Security                                                             
05 Administration's registered traveler programs; information provided under this                                          
06 paragraph shall be used for lawful purposes and is not limited to use within a program                                  
07 if the entity receives the information to facilitate driver's license or identification card                            
08 data verification and the individual has voluntarily provided the individual's driver's                                 
09       license or identification card.                                                                                   
10    * Sec. 14. AS 28.15.165(b) is amended to read:                                                                       
11 (b)  After reading the notice under (a) of this section, the law enforcement                                            
12 officer shall seize the person's physical driver's license if the physical driver's                                 
13 license [IT] is in the person's possession and shall deliver the physical driver's                                  
14 license [IT] to the department with a sworn report describing the circumstances under                                 
15 which the physical driver's license [IT] was seized. If the person was operating a                                    
16 commercial motor vehicle, the officer shall order the person out of service under                                       
17       AS 28.33.130.                                                                                                     
18    * Sec. 15. AS 28.15.166(a) is amended to read:                                                                       
19 (a)  A person who has received a notice under AS 28.15.165(a) may make a                                                
20 written request for administrative review of the department's action under                                              
21 AS 28.15.165(c) or for limited license privileges under AS 28.15.165(d). If the                                         
22 person's physical driver's license has not been previously surrendered to the                                         
23 department, the physical driver's license [IT] shall be surrendered to the department                                 
24       at the time the request for review is made.                                                                       
25    * Sec. 16. AS 28.15.182(d) is amended to read:                                                                       
26            (d)  When imposing a limitation under (c) of this section, the court shall                                   
27                 (1)  require the surrender of the physical driver's license; and                                      
28 (2)  issue to the person a certificate valid for the duration of the                                                    
29       limitation specifying the terms of the limited license.                                                           
30    * Sec. 17. AS 28.15.183(b) is amended to read:                                                                       
31 (b)  After reading the notice under (a) of this section, the peace officer shall                                        



01 seize the person's physical driver's license or permit if the physical driver's license                             
02 or permit [IT] is in the person's possession and shall deliver the physical driver's                                
03 license or permit [IT] to the department with a sworn report describing the                                           
04       circumstances under which the physical driver's license or permit [IT] was seized.                              
05    * Sec. 18. AS 28.15.184(a) is amended to read:                                                                       
06 (a)  A person who has received a notice under AS 28.15.183(a) may make a                                                
07 written request for administrative review of the department's action. If the person's                                   
08 physical driver's license or permit has not been previously surrendered to the                                        
09 department, the physical driver's license or permit [IT] shall be surrendered to the                                  
10       department at the time the request for review is made.                                                            
11    * Sec. 19. AS 28.15.187(b) is amended to read:                                                                       
12 (b)  After reading the notice under (a) of this section, the peace officer shall                                        
13 seize the person's physical driver's license if the physical driver's license [IT] is in                            
14 the person's possession and shall deliver the physical driver's license [IT] to the                                   
15 department with a sworn report describing the circumstances under which the                                           
16       physical driver's license [IT] was seized.                                                                      
17    * Sec. 20. AS 28.15.189(a) is amended to read:                                                                       
18 (a)  A person who has received a notice under AS 28.15.187(a) may make a                                                
19 written request for administrative review of the department's action. If the person's                                   
20 physical driver's license has not been previously surrendered to the department, the                                
21 physical driver's license [IT] shall be surrendered to the department at the time the                                 
22       request for review is made.                                                                                       
23    * Sec. 21. AS 28.15.191(c) is amended to read:                                                                       
24 (c)  A court that suspends, revokes, or limits a driver's license shall require the                                     
25 surrender of the physical license, and shall immediately forward the physical driver's                              
26 license [IT] to the department with the record of conviction and notification of the                                  
27 effective date of the suspension, revocation, or limitation as determined under                                         
28       AS 28.15.211(b).                                                                                                  
29    * Sec. 22. AS 28.15.191(g) is amended to read:                                                                       
30 (g)  A court that has ordered a person to refrain from consuming alcoholic                                              
31 beverages as part of a sentence for conviction of a crime under AS 28.35.030,                                           



01       28.35.032, or a similar municipal ordinance or as a condition of probation or parole                              
02       following a conviction under those sections or a similar municipal ordinance, or as a                             
03       condition of probation or parole for any other crime shall                                                        
04                 (1)  require the surrender of the person's physical license and                                       
05       identification card and forward the physical license and identification card to the                             
06       department;                                                                                                       
07                 (2)  report the order to the department within two days; and                                            
08                 (3)  inform the person that the person's license and identification card                                
09       are subject to cancellation under AS 28.15.161 and AS 18.65.310 and, if the person is                             
10 otherwise qualified to receive a license or identification card, when the person obtains                                
11 a new license or identification card, the license or identification card must list the                                  
12       restriction imposed by AS 04.16.160 for the period of probation or parole.                                        
13    * Sec. 23. AS 28.15.191(h) is amended to read:                                                                       
14 (h)  The board of parole shall notify the department within two days whenever                                           
15 a person has been ordered to refrain from consuming alcoholic beverages as a                                            
16 condition of parole, shall require the person to surrender the person's physical license                              
17 and identification card, and shall inform the person that the person's license and                                      
18 identification card are subject to cancellation under AS 28.15.161 and AS 18.65.310,                                    
19 and that, if the person is otherwise qualified to receive a license or identification card,                             
20 when the person obtains a new license or identification card, the license or                                            
21       identification card must list the restriction imposed by AS 04.16.160.                                            
22    * Sec. 24. AS 28.15.201(b) is amended to read:                                                                       
23            (b)  A court imposing a limitation under (a) of this section shall                                           
24                 (1)  require certification of employment;                                                               
25 (2)  require proof of enrollment in and compliance with or completion                                                   
26       of an alcoholism treatment program when appropriate;                                                              
27                 (3)  require the surrender of the physical driver's license; and                                      
28 (4)  issue to the licensee a certificate valid for the duration of the                                                  
29       limitation.                                                                                                       
30    * Sec. 25. AS 28.20.560(a) is amended to read:                                                                       
31 (a)  A person whose license or registration is suspended under any provision of                                         



01 this chapter, or whose policy of insurance or bond, when required under this chapter,                                   
02 is canceled or terminated, shall immediately return the person's physical license or                                  
03 registration to the department. If a person fails to return the license or registration to                              
04 the department, the department, through the commissioner of public safety, shall                                        
05 immediately direct a peace officer to obtain possession of the license or registration                                
06       [IT] and to return the license or registration [IT] to the department.

08            (d)  When imposing a limitation under this section, the department shall                                   
09                 (1)  require the surrender of the driver's physical license; and                                      
10 (2)  issue to the licensee a certificate valid for the duration of the                                                  
11       limitation.                                                                                                       
12    * Sec. 27. AS 28.33.100 is amended by adding a new subsection to read:                                               
13 (j)  Upon application, the department may issue an electronic commercial                                                
14       driver's license under AS 28.15.126.                                                                              
15    * Sec. 28. AS 28.33.140(i) is amended to read:                                                                       
16 (i)  In addition to the requirements of AS 28.15.191, a court that disqualifies a                                       
17 person from driving a commercial motor vehicle shall require the surrender of the                                       
18 physical license, and shall immediately forward the physical license to the department                              
19 with the record of conviction and notification of the effective date of the                                             
20 disqualification. If the disqualification occurs by administrative action as described in                               
21 (a) of this section, the person disqualified from driving shall surrender the physical                                
22       license to the department.                                                                                        
23    * Sec. 29. AS 28.35.034 is amended to read:                                                                          
24 Sec. 28.35.034. Surrender of license or permit. A person whose license or                                               
25 permit to operate or drive a motor vehicle has been revoked under AS 28.15.165 or                                       
26 28.15.181 shall surrender the physical license or permit to the department on receipt                                 
27 of notice of the revocation. After the period of revocation has expired, the person may                                 
28       make application for a new license as provided by law.                                                            
29    * Sec. 30. AS 33.16.150(i) is amended to read:                                                                       
30 (i)  In addition to other conditions of parole imposed under this section, for a                                        
31 prisoner who is serving a sentence for an offense involving the use of alcohol and                                      



01 whom the board has ordered to refrain from possessing or consuming alcoholic                                            
02 beverages, the board shall require the surrender of the person's physical driver's                                    
03 license or identification card, forward the physical license or identification card to the                            
04 department, and impose as a condition of parole that, if the parolee is eligible for a                                  
05 driver's license or identification card, the parolee shall apply to the department for a                                
06 new license or identification card with a restriction imposed on the person under                                       
07 AS 04.16.160. The board shall notify the department of the board's order under this                                     
08 subsection by providing a copy of the board's order. Upon discharge from parole, the                                    
09 board shall notify the department of the parolee's discharge. In this subsection,                                       
10       "department" means the Department of Administration.

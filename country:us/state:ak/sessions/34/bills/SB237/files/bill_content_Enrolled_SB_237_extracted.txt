AS 18.65.310(a) is amended to read: 07 (a) Upon payment of a $15 fee, the department shall issue a physical card 08 identical to the motor vehicle operator's license provided for in AS 28.15.111, except 09 that the card shall be of a different color and shall state in bold type letters across the 10 face of the card [IT] that the card [IT] is for identification purposes only. Upon 11 request of a person and upon payment of an additional $20 fee, the department may 12 issue an identification card under this section that is federally compliant. 13 * Sec. 2. AS 18.65.310 is amended by adding new subsections to read: 14 (s) Upon application, the department may issue an electronic identification 01 card to a person who possesses a physical identification card. A person issued an 02 electronic identification card shall retain the physical identification card issued by the 03 department. 04 (t) The department shall adopt regulations for the issuance and use of 05 electronic identification cards. 06 (u) Presentation of an electronic identification card on a mobile electronic 07 device for identification purposes does not constitute consent for a peace officer or 08 other authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety to search, view, or 09 access other contents of the mobile electronic device. A peace officer or other 10 authorized representative of the Department of Public Safety presented with an 11 electronic identification card shall promptly return the mobile electronic device to the 12 person once the peace officer or representative verifies the identity of the person. 13 (v) A peace officer or other authorized representative of the Department of 14 Public Safety presented with an electronic identification card on a mobile electronic 15 device under this section is immune from liability resulting from damage to the 16 device, except that a peace officer or other authorized representative of the 17 Department of Public Safety may be liable for civil damages that are the result of the 18 peace officer's or representative's intentional misconduct. 19 * Sec. 3. AS 18.65.320(b) is amended to read: 20 (b) A person whose identification card has been cancelled shall return the 21 physical card to the department. Failure to return a cancelled card within 10 days after 22 receiving notice of the cancellation is a violation punishable by a fine of up to $100. 23 * Sec. 4. AS 28.05.021 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 24 (c) The commissioner of administration may enter into an agreement with a 25 law enforcement agency, governmental agency or program, or data exchange service, 26 including the National Driver Register, the Problem Driver Pointer System, the 27 Commercial Driver's License Information System, the State-to-State Verification 28 Service, and the Driver's License Data Verification Service. 29 * Sec. 5. AS 28.05.051 is amended to read: 30 Sec. 28.05.051. Suspended or revoked documents. (a) When the department 31 suspends or revokes a vehicle registration, certificate of registration, registration plate, 01 permit, or certificate of title or suspends, cancels, or revokes a license, the owner or 02 person in possession of the document shall, immediately upon receiving notice of the 03 suspension, revocation, or cancellation, mail or deliver the registration, certificate, 04 plate, permit, or physical license to the department. 05 (b) The commissioner, officers and employees of the department designated 06 by the commissioner, judges and employees of a court, and all peace officers [,] may 07 take possession of a certificate of title, registration, or physical license issued by this 08 jurisdiction that has been revoked, canceled, limited, or suspended, or is fictitious, 09 stolen, or altered. 10 * Sec. 6. AS 28.05.068(a) is amended to read: 11 (a) If the department conveys, distributes, or communicates data to be used in 12 a database, index, pointer system, or any other system managed by an entity other than 13 the department [, INCLUDING THE AMERICAN ASSOCIATION OF MOTOR 14 VEHICLE ADMINISTRATORS,] to comply with the requirements of P.L. 109-13, 15 Division B (REAL ID Act of 2005), the department may not convey, distribute, or 16 communicate to that entity any data not required to be conveyed, distributed, or 17 communicated for the state to be certified by the United States Department of 18 Homeland Security to be in compliance with the requirements of P.L. 109-13, 19 Division B (REAL ID Act of 2005). 20 * Sec. 7. AS 28.05.068(b) is amended to read: 21 (b) Notwithstanding (a) of this section, the department may [NOT] convey, 22 distribute, or communicate data to a private entity only to confirm the information 23 of an individual who has voluntarily provided the individual's state driver's 24 license or identification card [ANY SCANNED OR STORED DOCUMENTS 25 COLLECTED IN ORDER] to carry out the provisions of AS 28.15.151(g) 26 [AS 28.15]. 27 * Sec. 8. AS 28.15.011(b) is amended to read: 28 (b) Every person exercising the person's privilege to drive, or exercising any 29 degree of physical control of a motor vehicle on [UPON] a highway, vehicular way or 30 area, or other public property in this state, is required to have in the possession of the 31 person a valid physical or electronic Alaska driver's license issued under the 01 provisions of this chapter for the type or class of vehicle driven, unless expressly 02 exempted by law from this requirement. 03 * Sec. 9. AS 28.15 is amended by adding a new section to read: 04 Sec. 28.15.126. Electronic licenses and permits. The commissioner 11 may adopt regulations to require a driver to possess a physical driver's license on the 12 driver's person in certain circumstances. 13 (c) If a person's physical driver's license is seized, revoked, canceled, limited, 14 restricted, or suspended under this chapter, the department shall display a notice 15 regarding the seizure, revocation, cancellation, limitation, restriction, or suspension 16 across the front of the person's electronic driver's license. 17 * Sec. 10. AS 28.15.131(a) is amended to read: 18 (a) A licensee shall have the licensee's physical or electronic driver's license 19 in immediate possession at all times when driving a motor vehicle, and shall present 20 the license for inspection upon the demand of a peace officer or other authorized 21 representative of the Department of Public Safety identified as such to the licensee by 22 the officer or representative. However, a person charged with violating this section 23 may not be convicted if the person produces in court or in the office of the arresting or 24 citing officer, a driver's license previously issued to the person that was valid at the 25 time of the person's arrest or citation. 26 * Sec. 11. AS 33.16.150(i) is amended to read: 30 (i) In addition to other conditions of parole imposed under this section, for a 31 prisoner who is serving a sentence for an offense involving the use of alcohol and 01 whom the board has ordered to refrain from possessing or consuming alcoholic 02 beverages, the board shall require the surrender of the person's physical driver's 03 license or identification card, forward the physical license or identification card to the 04 department, and impose as a condition of parole that, if the parolee is eligible for a 05 driver's license or identification card, the parolee shall apply to the department for a 06 new license or identification card with a restriction imposed on the person under 07 AS 04.16.160. The board shall notify the department of the board's order under this 08 subsection by providing a copy of the board's order. Upon discharge from parole, the 09 board shall notify the department of the parolee's discharge. In this subsection, 10 "department" means the Department of Administration. 11 * Sec. 31.
